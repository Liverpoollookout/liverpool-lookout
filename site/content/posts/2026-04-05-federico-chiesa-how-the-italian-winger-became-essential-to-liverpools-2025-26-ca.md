# Federico Chiesa: How the Italian Winger Became Essential to Liverpool's 2025-26 Campaign

Federico Chiesa's arrival at Liverpool has quietly become one of the most astute additions to Arne Slot's squad, particularly as the Reds navigate a challenging 2025-26 campaign marked by inconsistency and mounting pressure following recent high-profile defeats.

With Mohamed Salah and Luis Diaz forming the primary attacking thrust on the flanks, Chiesa has carved out a distinctive niche within Slot's tactical framework—one that extends far beyond simple wing-play. The Italian international brings a nuanced understanding of positioning, intelligent movement in congested spaces, and the technical proficiency to unlock defences when Liverpool's more direct approaches falter.

## Tactical Integration and Role

Slot has orchestrated Chiesa's role with considerable subtlety. Rather than asking the 28-year-old to replicate Salah's explosive directness or Diaz's relentless pressing intensity, the manager has positioned Chiesa as a tactical variable—a player capable of drifting into midfield to receive possession in deeper areas, thereby creating numerical advantages during build-up play.

This flexibility has proven particularly valuable given Liverpool's midfield transitions this season. With Dominik Szoboszlai, Alexis Mac Allister, and Ryan Gravenberch managing a heavier creative burden than previous campaigns, Chiesa's ability to operate as a secondary playmaker has reduced the creative dependency on any single midfielder.

Against possession-dominant opponents, Chiesa's positioning offers Liverpool greater structural balance. He frequently drops deep to support the transition, creating passing options for Virgil van Dijk and the centre-back partnership whilst simultaneously maintaining attacking threat—a dual responsibility that few contemporary wingers execute with consistency.

## Technical Strengths and Capabilities

Chiesa's left foot remains a considerable asset, particularly in crossing scenarios where he can deliver quality service to Darwin Nunez and Diogo Jota. His ability to cut inside and create space for central actions provides Liverpool with attacking patterns that complement Salah's right-sided dominance.

The Italian's first touch remains elite-level, enabling him to operate effectively in tight defensive schemes where Slot's pressing intensity generates congested midfield zones. His vision—often underestimated by observers focused solely on his goalscoring output—allows Liverpool to execute quicker combinations when opportunities present themselves.

What distinguishes Chiesa in Slot's system is his willingness to make the difficult play rather than the obvious one. Where younger wingers might force passes into Nunez or attempt immediate crosses, Chiesa frequently demonstrates the patience to maintain possession through secondary movements, inviting teammates into more advantageous positions.

## Recent Form and Season Context

The 2025-26 campaign has presented considerable obstacles for Liverpool as a collective, with recent defeats to Manchester City and FA Cup disappointments dampening the optimism from earlier phases of the season. Chiesa has not been exempt from these struggles, yet his underlying performance metrics suggest sustained quality despite limited goal contributions.

His work-rate off the ball remains exceptional. Chiesa consistently ranks among Liverpool's leading presser categories, applying pressure to opposition defenders and forcing turnovers in advanced areas. This defensive responsibility, often overlooked in player assessment, validates Slot's continued deployment despite more glamorous alternatives within the squad.

## Areas for Development

Chiesa's injury history throughout his career warrants cautious management; Liverpool's medical team has necessarily adopted a conservative approach to his minutes, particularly given the fixture congestion inherent in English football.

Whilst his crossing has improved noticeably under Slot's coaching, consistency in the final third remains an area for refinement. Chiesa's expected goals ratio suggests he occasionally overwrought situations where simpler solutions—direct passes or immediate crosses—would prove more efficient. Developing greater ruthlessness in transition moments would elevate his overall contribution.

Additionally, his integration with Nunez could yield greater chemistry. The pair's understanding of each other's positioning remains developing, occasionally resulting in mistimed movements where coordinated runs might generate clearer chances.

## The Fan Perspective and Future Outlook

Liverpool supporters have increasingly appreciated Chiesa's contribution beyond statistics. His intelligent movement, defensive commitment, and creative intelligence represent qualities essential for sustained competition at the highest level—qualities becoming increasingly valuable as Slot reshapes the squad following departures including Trent Alexander-Arnold's summer 2024 move to Real Madrid.

As Liverpool pursue recovery from recent setbacks, Chiesa's role may increase considerably. His tactical intelligence offers Slot defensive stability and creative unpredictability—a combination particularly valuable during pressure periods where systematic breakdowns become apparent.

The Italian's continued presence at Anfield suggests a player finally discovering the platform suited to his considerable technical abilities. Whether he becomes a cornerstone of Liverpool's future success will depend on both injury management and his capacity to translate intelligence into greater goal-contribution output.

For now, Federico Chiesa represents exactly the type of multifaceted talent necessary for modern elite football—a player whose impact extends far beyond conventional statistics, yet whose development towards increased clinical finishing could prove transformative for Liverpool's ambitions.
