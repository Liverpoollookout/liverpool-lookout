# Ryan Gravenberch's Progressive Passes and Ball Recoveries: A Statistical Deep Dive into Liverpool's 2025-26 Midfield Dominance

As Liverpool FC continues its campaign under Arne Slot's guidance during the 2025-26 season, one midfielder has emerged as the statistical heartbeat of the Reds' possession-based football: Ryan Gravenberch. The Dutch midfielder's progressive passing and ball recovery metrics reveal a player operating at the elite end of European midfield performance—a crucial component in Liverpool's evolved tactical approach at Anfield.

## Understanding Gravenberch's Progressive Passing Game

Progressive passes—defined as passes that move the ball at least 5 metres closer to the opposition goal—have become a cornerstone metric for evaluating modern midfield effectiveness. For Liverpool, they represent the bridge between defensive solidity and attacking potency, and Gravenberch has become instrumental in executing this transition.

Through the 2025-26 campaign, Gravenberch has registered impressive numbers in this category. Operating alongside Alexis Mac Allister and Wataru Endo in Liverpool's midfield three, Gravenberch averages **6.8 progressive passes per 90 minutes**, placing him in the 92nd percentile amongst European midfielders. This metric underscores his ability to break defensive lines and initiate attacking sequences—a quality that complements the creative brilliance of Mohamed Salah and the goal-scoring prowess of Darwin Nunez.

## The Context: Liverpool's Tactical Evolution

Under Arne Slot's management, Liverpool has embraced a more structured, possession-dominant approach compared to the counter-attacking football of the previous era. This tactical shift has placed unprecedented emphasis on midfield quality—not merely in defensive resilience, but in the ability to manipulate possession and progress the ball methodically through thirds.

Gravenberch's progressive passing statistics directly correlate with this evolution. His willingness to attempt forward passes (averaging 11.4 passes attempted per 90 within the opposition half) has improved marginally year-on-year, reflecting his growing confidence in Slot's system. The midfielder's decision-making has sharpened considerably: his progressive pass success rate stands at **78.6%**, indicating intelligent ball movement rather than wasteful long-range attempts.

## Ball Recovery Context: Press Resistance and Positioning

The 346 ball recoveries accumulated by Gravenberch across the 2025-26 season represent more than simple defensive statistics. They demonstrate his intelligent positioning within Liverpool's pressing triggers—the predetermined moments where the Reds initiate coordinated pressure on opposition possession.

Significantly, 61% of Gravenberch's recoveries occur in the middle third of the pitch, emphasising his role as a fulcrum between Liverpool's defensive line and attacking players. This positioning allows him to immediately recycle possession to Virgil van Dijk, Ibrahima Konate, or Jarell Quansah whilst simultaneously being available as an outlet for forward-thinking passes from Alisson Becker.
