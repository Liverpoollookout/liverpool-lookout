# Liverpool Face Major Summer Overhaul: Salah Departure Looms as Slot Plans Squad Reshape

**Thursday, 26 March 2026**

Liverpool FC faces a significant summer of transition, with Mohamed Salah's anticipated departure setting the tone for what promises to be a comprehensive squad reshaping under manager Arne Slot.

## Salah's Exit Confirmed for Summer

According to reports, Mohamed Salah is set to leave Liverpool at the end of the 2025-26 season, bringing an end to an extraordinary eight-year tenure at Anfield. The Egyptian winger, who has been integral to the club's recent success, will depart when his contract expires this summer.

Salah's exit represents the most significant departure since Trent Alexander-Arnold's move to Real Madrid last summer. The loss of his goal-scoring prowess—particularly his devastating form on the right flank—will necessitate strategic recruitment from Slot's team.

## Rebuilding the Attack

With Salah's departure confirmed, Liverpool must reinforce their attacking options. The club currently possesses attacking quality in Luis Diaz, Darwin Nunez, Cody Gakpo, and the recently acquired Federico Chiesa, yet the creative burden previously shared by Salah will require compensation.

Slot's recruitment strategy will likely focus on identifying a world-class right-winger capable of matching Salah's creative and finishing standards. Sources suggest the club may explore both established talents and emerging prospects during the summer window.

## Midfield Considerations

The midfield department remains relatively settled, with Dominik Szoboszlai, Ryan Gravenberch, Alexis Mac Allister, and Curtis Jones providing a solid foundation. However, it is claimed that Slot may seek additional depth in this area, particularly following the departures of veteran midfielders in recent seasons including Thiago Alcantara, Jordan Henderson, and Fabinho.

Wataru Endo and Harvey Elliott offer useful alternatives, though reinforcement could be considered prudent given Liverpool's ambitions across multiple competitions.

## Defence Remains Solid

Liverpool's defensive core appears stable for the 2025-26 season. Virgil van Dijk continues as captain and world-class centre-back, with Ibrahima Konate, Joe Gomez, and Jarell Quansah providing capable alternatives. The full-back positions see Conor Bradley and Konstantinos Tsimikas supporting at left-back, following Alexander-Arnold's Real Madrid transfer.

Alisson Becker remains exceptional in goal, with Caoimhin Kelleher offering trusted backup.

## Potential Outgoings Beyond Salah

While Salah's departure is confirmed, sources suggest the club may consider additional strategic exits as Slot shapes his squad according to specific tactical requirements. The managerial philosophy adopted since Slot's arrival has prompted evaluation of all squad members.

No other major departures have been confirmed, though it is claimed that several fringe players may seek opportunities elsewhere should they fail to secure regular first-team football at Anfield.

## Summer Priorities

Slot's recruitment focus will likely concentrate on:

- **Right-wing replacement** for Salah, representing the primary priority
- **Midfield reinforcement**, particularly if any unexpected departures occur
- **Attacking depth**, given injuries throughout the 2025-26 campaign
- **Squad balance**, ensuring competitive options across all positions

## Financial Considerations

Salah's departure will free significant financial resources, potentially enabling ambitious recruitment. Liverpool's summer transfer budget should be substantially boosted, allowing Slot to pursue targets previously considered outside reach.

Reported fee structures and wage savings will provide flexibility to address multiple positions during a potentially transformative summer window.

## Conclusion

Liverpool enters a transitional period, with Mohamed Salah's departure marking a clear watershed moment. Arne Slot faces the challenge—and opportunity—of reshaping an attack that has dominated under previous regimes. The manager's summer decisions will prove crucial in maintaining Anfield's competitive standards.
