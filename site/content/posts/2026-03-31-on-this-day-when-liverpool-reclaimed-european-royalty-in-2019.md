# On This Day: When Liverpool Reclaimed European Royalty in 2019

As we sit on the cusp of a new era under Arne Slot, it feels fitting to reflect on one of the most transformative moments in modern Liverpool FC history. Six years ago this season—on 1 June 2019—the Reds secured their sixth European Cup, ending a 14-year drought and announcing their return as genuine continental contenders.

## The Road to Madrid

Under Jurgen Klopp's stewardship, Liverpool had already rebuilt themselves into a formidable force domestically. Yet the 2018-19 Champions League represented something more significant: vindication that the German manager's philosophy could compete at the highest level of European football.

The path to the Wanda Metropolitano in Madrid was dramatic and, at times, torturous. Liverpool's journey included that unforgettable night at Anfield against Barcelona—a moment that transcended football. Trailing 3-0 from the first leg, few gave the Reds a realistic chance. Yet Klopp's side dismantled the Spanish giants 4-0, with Mohamed Salah delivering a masterclass that reminded everyone why the Egyptian winger had captured Merseyside's heart.

Salah's ascent had been meteoric. From his arrival in summer 2017, deemed a gamble by some, the 26-year-old had become Liverpool's talisman. His blistering pace, intelligent movement, and clinical finishing made him virtually unplayable on his day. Against Barcelona, he epitomised everything Klopp's system demanded: relentless, intelligent, and utterly devastating.

## The Final: Poetry in Motion

The Champions League final itself, whilst not a tactical masterpiece, showcased Liverpool's resilience and character. Tottenham Hotspur provided stern opposition, but it was Mohamed Salah who settled the contest early, capitalising on a hesitant Mauricio Pochettino defensive setup. Later, Divock Origi—that cult hero substitute—sealed the victory with a finish that would define his Anfield legacy.

Alisson Becker, the Brazilian goalkeeper recruited in summer 2018 for a world-record fee, had proven instrumental throughout the competition. His presence commanded the box; his distribution initiated attacks. Virgil van Dijk, who had arrived in January 2018, marshalled the defence with an imperious authority that was becoming familiar to Anfield regulars.

## More Than Silverware

Yet this triumph transcended the trophy itself. For a club whose recent history had been marked by near-misses and heartbreak, the 2019 Champions League represented psychological liberation. Liverpool were no longer the romantic underdogs; they were genuine elite-level operators.

The domestic implications proved equally seismic. Having fallen short in the league race that season, Klopp's side responded by winning 19 consecutive league matches the following campaign—a Premier League record at the time. That 2019-20 season would bring the long-awaited Premier League title, ending a 30-year drought.

Mohamed Salah's role in this rejuvenation cannot be overstated. His goal-scoring consistency, his willingness to embrace Klopp's high-pressing demands, and his ability to produce in crucial moments made him the defining player of this era. Alongside Salah, players like van Dijk and Alisson Becker formed the bedrock upon which Liverpool rebuilt their empire.

## Legacy and Reflection

As we move into the 2025-26 season under Arne Slot, with Mohamed Salah still performing at elite level, it's worth remembering that 2019 moment. That Champions League triumph represented not merely a return to European glory but a statement of intent: Liverpool were back.

The squad has evolved considerably since then. Virgil van Dijk remains, that colossus in defence. Alisson Becker continues between the posts. But Salah remains the spiritual continuation of that winning mentality—that relentless pursuit of excellence that defines Anfield in the modern era.

The departures of Trent Alexander-Arnold, Thiago Alcantara, and others represent natural evolution. Yet the core philosophy—the pressing, the intensity, the refusal to accept mediocrity—persists. Under Slot's guidance, this Liverpool side seeks to write new chapters.

But on this day in late March 2026, when looking back at 2019, supporters can take solace. That magical European night in Madrid wasn't a fleeting moment of glory. It was the beginning of Liverpool's restoration as a force capable of winning at the very highest level—a legacy that continues to inspire this new generation at Anfield.

Six years on, that sixth European Cup remains a beacon of what Liverpool FC can achieve when brilliance, belief, and strategy converge.
