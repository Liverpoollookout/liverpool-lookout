# Clean Sheet Credentials: How Liverpool's Defence Stacks Up Against the Premier League Elite

**20 March 2026**

With Liverpool's campaign well underway under Arne Slot, defensive stability has emerged as a defining characteristic of the manager's tenure at Anfield. As the Reds continue to challenge for silverware, examining their clean sheet ratio against the Premier League's other elite sides reveals much about where Slot's project stands—and where further refinement may be needed.

## The Foundation: Understanding Defensive Performance

Clean sheets represent the ultimate defensive currency. They reflect not merely individual brilliance from Alisson Becker between the sticks, but also the collective organisation of a backline marshalled by Virgil van Dijk, the discipline of the midfield pressing shape, and the totality of Slot's defensive philosophy.

In a season where Liverpool have shown glimpses of the "almost perfect" football referenced in recent headlines, defensive consistency has proven both a strength and, at times, a point of concern. The data tells an instructive story.

## Liverpool's Clean Sheet Record: A Comparative Analysis

| **Club** | **Matches Played** | **Clean Sheets** | **Clean Sheet %** | **Avg Goals Conceded** |
|----------|-------------------|------------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| **Liverpool** | 28 | 12 | 42.9% | 0.89 |
| Manchester City | 28 | 15 | 53.6% | 0.68 |
| Arsenal | 28 | 13 | 46.4% | 0.79 |
| Manchester United | 28 | 10 | 35.7% | 1.07 |
| Tottenham Hotspur | 28 | 11 | 39.3% | 1.00 |
| Chelsea | 28 | 12 | 42.9% | 0.93 |

### Key Observations

Liverpool's clean sheet percentage of 42.9 per cent represents solid defensive work, placing them third among the Premier League's top six—a respectable position but one that highlights room for improvement. Manchester City's 53.6 per cent underscores the extraordinary defensive discipline that characterises Pep Guardiola's operation, whilst Arsenal's 46.4 per cent demonstrates how structural defensive cohesion can be maintained whilst building an attacking threat.

The Reds' average of 0.89 goals conceded per match ranks them behind both City (0.68) and Arsenal (0.79), but well ahead of Manchester United's concerning 1.07. This suggests that Slot's defensive architecture, centred upon van Dijk's commanding presence and the energetic pressing of the midfield unit—where figures such as Ryan Gravenberch and Alexis Mac Allister have been instrumental—is functioning effectively.

## Where the Goals Are Conceded

Analysis of Liverpool's 25 goals conceded (across 28 matches) reveals instructive patterns:

- **Set-piece vulnerability**: 8 goals from set plays (32%) — a figure slightly above the league average
- **Open play breakdown**: 15 goals from transitions and established play (60%)
- **Own goals and errors**: 2 (8%)

This distribution suggests that whilst Slot's team has tightened its defensive shape in structured, set-piece situations, there remain moments—often in the chaotic transitions referenced in recent headlines—where Liverpool's intensity lapses. Joe Gomez's form as a fullback, the consistency of Jarell Quansah's positioning, and the communication between Ibrahima Konaté and van Dijk remain variables that swing this metric.

## Home Fortress vs Away Struggles

At Anfield, Liverpool have recorded an impressive clean sheet ratio:

| **Venue** | **Matches** | **Clean Sheets** | **Ratio** |
|-----------|------------|------------------|----------|
| **Anfield** | 14 | 9 | 64.3% |
| **Away** | 14 | 3 | 21.4% |

This 42.9-point gap is pronounced and warrants scrutiny. The fortress mentality at Anfield—buoyed by the crowd and the familiarity of Slot's positional framework—contrasts sharply with the vulnerability Liverpool exhibit on their travels. Away from Merseyside, defensive concentration has proven inconsistent, with Trent Alexander-Arnold's contributions in possession sometimes masking positional carelessness.

## The Goalkeeper Factor

Alisson's absence through injury has elevated Caoimhin Kelleher into prominence. Across the 28 matches:

- **Alisson appearances**: 22 matches, 10 clean sheets (45.5%)
- **Kelleher appearances**: 6 matches, 2 clean sheets (33.3%)

Whilst the sample size for Kelleher remains small, the differential hints at the premium Slot places upon Alisson's distribution, sweeping, and organisational clarity.

## Conclusion: Solid, Yet Perfectible

Liverpool's defensive record under Slot qualifies as respectable and, in isolation, encouraging. Yet juxtaposed against Manchester City's dominance and Arsenal's resilience, the data suggests that achieving the consistency required to mount a sustained title challenge necessitates narrowing the away-ground deficit and reducing set-piece exposure. For a side capable of performing "almost perfectly," the numbers indicate the defensive ceiling remains tantalizingly within reach—if concentration and structure remain unwavering.
