# Why Virgil van Dijk Deserves Your Ballon d'Or Vote

As Liverpool's magnificent 4-0 demolition of Galatasaray at Anfield demonstrated once again, there remains a glaring imbalance in how we celebrate footballing excellence. Whilst Mohamed Salah glided through the Turkish side's defence with customary brilliance, it was the commanding presence of Virgil van Dijk that made such attacking freedom possible. Yet when the Ballon d'Or conversation emerges each November, the captain's name rarely features with the prominence it deserves. This needs to change.

Let us be abundantly clear: Van Dijk is not a fashionable award winner. He does not accumulate highlight-reel assists or score the dramatic injury-time goals that dominate social media. The Dutchman's art is far more subtle—and infinitely more valuable. In the 2025-26 campaign, under Arne Slot's stewardship, Van Dijk has orchestrated Liverpool's defensive excellence with the precision of a conductor leading a symphony. Every clearance is decisive. Every interception perfectly timed. Every distribution measured and purposeful.

Consider the architecture of modern elite football. A team's defensive foundation determines its ceiling. Liverpool's recent resurgence under Slot has been predicated on the security Van Dijk and Ibrahima Konate provide. When your centre-back pairing eliminates the chaos that forces your goalkeeper—even one as exceptional as Alisson Becker—into desperate saves, you grant your attacking talents the canvas upon which to paint masterpieces. Salah, Luis Diaz, and Darwin Nunez thrive because Van Dijk ensures opposing attacks rarely materialise into genuine threats.

The counter-argument is predictable: "Defenders cannot win the Ballon d'Or because attacking output is more measurable." This reasoning, frankly, represents a fundamental failure of football analysis. A defender's impact permeates every phase of play. Van Dijk's passing range initiates transitions. His aerial dominance prevents set-piece disasters. His leadership marshals ten outfield players into cohesive units. These contributions are quantifiable for anyone willing to look beyond goal tallies.

Moreover, Van Dijk's consistency transcends individual seasons. Since arriving at Anfield, he has maintained elite standards in a way few defenders—perhaps no defenders—have managed across such a sustained period. Injuries have occasionally interrupted his presence, yet even compromised versions of Van Dijk outperform most fully-fit centre-backs. This season, at an age when many defenders decline, he has been immaculate. The Galatasaray performance was merely the latest confirmation of his undiminished excellence.

Yes, other players merit consideration. Salah has been extraordinary; his goal-scoring continues to defy age. Dominik Szoboszlai's Renaissance under Slot suggests a midfielder finally fulfilling his prodigious potential. Alexis Mac Allister provides industry and intelligence. But none of these factors should obscure the fundamental truth: Liverpool's title challenge, their Champions League progression, their entire competitive structure rests upon the shoulders of a player whose defensive brilliance has become so routine that it risks invisibility.

The Ballon d'Or should recognise football's best player, not merely its most prolific goal-scorer. By that metric, Van Dijk belongs in the conversation alongside any peer in world football. His performance against Galatasaray, where he neutralised threat after threat whilst orchestrating play from deep, exemplified a defender operating at levels other centre-backs simply cannot reach. When we discuss Liverpool's renaissance under Slot, we celebrate attacking verve and midfield control. But underlying everything is Van Dijk's unwavering excellence.

It is time the football community recognised this. A Ballon d'Or vote for Virgil van Dijk would not represent sentimental acknowledgement of past glories. It would reflect clear-eyed assessment of current supremacy in football's most demanding defensive position. At Anfield, under Slot's management, Van Dijk remains the foundation upon which champions are built.

He deserves recognition accordingly.
