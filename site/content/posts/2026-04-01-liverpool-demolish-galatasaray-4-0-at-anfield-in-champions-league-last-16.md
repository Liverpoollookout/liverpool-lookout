# Liverpool Demolish Galatasaray 4-0 at Anfield in Champions League Last 16

Liverpool FC produced a devastating European performance to dispose of Turkish champions Galatasaray with a commanding 4-0 victory in the UEFA Champions League Last 16 first leg at Anfield on Wednesday evening. The result represents a near-perfect night for Arne Slot's side and positions them perfectly ahead of the return fixture in Istanbul.

From the opening whistle, Liverpool controlled proceedings with the kind of intensity and tactical precision that has defined their 2025-26 campaign under Slot. Galatasaray, despite their domestic pedigree, found themselves overwhelmed by the Reds' relentless pressing and creative attacking play, with the home defence—marshalled superbly by Virgil van Dijk and Ibrahima Konate—rarely troubled by the visitors' limited attacking threat.

## First Half Dominance

Liverpool's attacking intent was clear from the start, and they broke the deadlock with clinical efficiency. Mohamed Salah, operating in his familiar right-sided role, was at the heart of Liverpool's attacking play throughout, combining defensive discipline with his trademark cutting edge in the final third.

The opening goal came within the first twenty minutes, establishing the tone for what would become a one-sided affair. Slot's team pressed high, forcing Galatasaray into uncomfortable positions and capitalising on their defensive frailties. The Turkish side's midfield, lacking the quality to match Liverpool's engine room, found themselves consistently second to loose balls.

Dominik Szoboszlai and Alexis Mac Allister controlled the tempo in the centre of the pitch, with the latter particularly impressive in breaking up play and transitioning Liverpool into attack. Ryan Gravenberch's presence provided additional ballast, allowing the Reds to suffocate Galatasaray's creative outlets.

By the interval, Liverpool had established complete dominance, and a two-goal advantage reflected their superior quality and execution. Alisson Becker, commanding in goal, had barely been called upon, such was Liverpool's command of the match.

## Second Half Procession

If the first half suggested a comfortable evening, the second period proved to be little more than a procession. Liverpool maintained their intensity without ever appearing flustered, methodically extending their advantage. Slot made tactical adjustments to protect key players and rotate his squad mindful of upcoming domestic commitments, but the Reds never relinquished control.

Darwin Nunez, leading the line with characteristic hunger and work ethic, contributed significantly to Liverpool's suffocating pressure, whilst Luis Díaz's pace and directness on the left flank continued to trouble Galatasaray's depleted backline. Federico Chiesa, operating in support of the attacking triumvirate, added technical quality and defensive responsibility.

The third goal arrived with the kind of inevitability this performance had suggested from the opening minutes, followed shortly by a fourth that left no doubt about the gulf in class between these two European heavyweights. Each addition to the scoreline was met with roars from an Anfield crowd that sensed they were witnessing a European performance of genuine quality from their side.

## Defensive Solidity

Whilst the attacking performance captured headlines, Liverpool's defensive organisation deserves equal commendation. Van Dijk, the captain and embodiment of Liverpool's backline, read the game with his customary intelligence, whilst Konate provided physical presence and composure alongside him. The full-back positions—occupied with defensive acumen—ensured Galatasaray possessed no outlet down the flanks.

Alisson, afforded minimal action, remained concentrated and alert throughout, a representation of Liverpool's collective focus despite the comfortable scoreline.

## Slot's Reaction

Arne Slot's demeanour throughout reflected the professionalism demanded at Liverpool. The Dutchman would have been satisfied with the intensity of his team's pressing, the incisiveness of their attacking play, and their ability to maintain standards despite the comfortable margin. This is precisely the type of dominant European performance that builds momentum in continental competition, and Liverpool will travel to Istanbul in a fortnight harbouring genuine confidence of progressing further.

With significant injury concerns potentially affecting other contenders and PSG's domestic fixture juggling creating complications in their schedule, Liverpool's emphatic display serves notice that they represent genuine Champions League contenders this season.

## Looking Ahead

The return fixture in Istanbul presents fewer complications now, though Slot will counsel against complacency. Galatasaray, despite their diminished showing at Anfield, retain dangerous attacking talents and the prospect of a hostile atmosphere in their own stadium cannot be dismissed entirely. However, with a four-goal cushion and a squad brimming with confidence, Liverpool possess all the advantages for the closing chapter of this tie.

For now, though, Liverpool can reflect on an evening that encapsulated the very best of what Slot's system produces: attacking fluidity, defensive discipline, midfield control, and an intensity that suffocates opposition. Against a Turkish side that might have harboured hopes of a competitive fixture, Liverpool delivered a masterclass in European football.
