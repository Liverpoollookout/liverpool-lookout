# Liverpool Transfer Roundup: Salah Departure Signals Anfield Rebuild Under Slot

**Sunday, 29 March 2026**

Mohamed Salah's impending departure from Liverpool FC has dominated the transfer landscape this week, signalling the beginning of a significant squad overhaul at Anfield under manager Arne Slot. The Egyptian winger's decision to leave at season's end marks the end of an era at Merseyside, prompting urgent discussions regarding potential replacements and midfield reinforcements heading into the 2025-26 campaign.

## Salah's Exit Confirmed

According to reports, Mohamed Salah has informed Liverpool of his intention to leave the club when his contract expires this summer. The 34-year-old has been a cornerstone of the Reds' attacking play for nearly a decade, and his departure will leave a considerable void on the right flank that Slot must address swiftly.

With Luis Diaz established on the left wing and Darwin Nunez leading the line, Liverpool will require a world-class replacement to maintain their competitive edge. Sources suggest the club has already begun scouting alternatives, though no formal targets have been confirmed by the Anfield hierarchy.

## Midfield Reinforcements Priority

Amine Slot's tactical preferences have become increasingly clear during his tenure. The manager favours dynamic, ball-carrying midfielders who can press aggressively and link play between defence and attack. With Ryan Gravenberch, Alexis Mac Allister, and Wataru Endo forming the engine room, Liverpool's recruitment team is understood to be exploring additional depth.

Curtis Jones and Harvey Elliott remain promising homegrown talents, yet according to reports, Slot believes supplementary investment in the centre of midfield would strengthen the squad's resilience in Europe's elite competitions.

## Defensive Stability Maintained

On the positive front, Virgil van Dijk, Ibrahima Konate, and Joe Gomez provide defensive solidity at the heart of Liverpool's backline. Caoimhin Kelleher has proven a more-than-capable deputy to Alisson Becker, easing concerns about the goalkeeper position.

However, sources suggest Liverpool may explore left-back reinforcements, with Konstantinos Tsimikas and Conor Bradley competing for the role vacated by Trent Alexander-Arnold's summer 2024 move to Real Madrid. The recovery of Bradley from injury remains crucial to Slot's planning.

## Federico Chiesa's Integration

Federico Chiesa's recent arrival offers encouragement regarding the club's ability to attract quality talent. The Italian winger provides additional creativity and experience to complement Luis Diaz's directness. Yet it is claimed that Slot may seek further attacking alternatives if substantial bids arrive for any of Liverpool's forward contingent.

## Summer Transfer Strategy

It is claimed that Liverpool's summer strategy will centre on three pillars: securing a replacement for Salah, bolstering midfield options, and maintaining defensive depth. The club's financial position remains strong, though any major departures beyond Salah remain unconfirmed.

Diogo Jota, Cody Gakpo, and Darwin Nunez should form the core of Liverpool's attacking threat, with opportunities for squad rotation preventing reliance on any single player.

## Conclusion

Whilst Salah's departure concludes a remarkable chapter in Liverpool FC's recent history, it presents Arne Slot with an opportunity to reshape the squad according to his footballing philosophy. Sources suggest the manager possesses both the ambition and tactical acumen to guide the Reds through this transition period.

With the summer transfer window approaching, Anfield insiders expect significant activity as Liverpool FC charts its course for sustained success under Slot's stewardship.
