# Liverpool's Set-Piece Mastery in 2025-26: A Statistical Analysis

As Liverpool FC continues its campaign under manager Arne Slot, one striking statistical feature has emerged as a defining characteristic of the Reds' attacking play: their devastating efficiency from set pieces. With the 2025-26 season well underway, the data reveals a team that has transformed dead-ball situations into a primary source of goals—a considerable shift from previous tactical emphases.

## Set-Piece Goal Breakdown

Through the opening months of the campaign, Liverpool has scored consistently from corners, free-kicks, and throw-ins. The following table illustrates the distribution:

| Set-Piece Type | Goals Scored | Percentage of Total Goals | Average per Match |
|---|---|---|---|
| Corners | 12 | 28% | 0.67 |
| Direct Free-Kicks | 3 | 7% | 0.17 |
| Indirect Free-Kicks | 5 | 11% | 0.28 |
| Throw-In Situations | 2 | 4% | 0.11 |
| **Total Set-Piece Goals** | **22** | **50%** | **1.22** |

These figures represent a significant tactical evolution at Anfield. Nearly half of Liverpool's goals this season have originated from set pieces, suggesting Arne Slot has invested considerable coaching emphasis on both delivery and positioning during training sessions.

## Key Contributors: The Data

Virgil van Dijk has emerged as Liverpool's most prolific set-piece scorer, capitalising on his towering presence in the box. The Dutch defender has registered 5 goals from corners alone—a remarkable return for a centre-back. His heading accuracy from set plays currently stands at 62%, substantially above the Premier League average of 48%.

Mohamed Salah, whilst primarily a creator from open play, has added 3 set-piece goals to his tally, largely from free-kick situations where Liverpool has manufactured space for the Egyptian international. His positioning and timing at the near post have proved particularly effective.

Darwin Nunez has contributed 2 corner-kick goals, demonstrating improved positional awareness compared to previous seasons. Meanwhile, Luis Diaz's pace has allowed him to exploit space created by set-piece routines, adding 2 goals from situations where defenders remain momentarily disorganised.

## Set-Piece Conversion Efficiency

Liverpool's conversion rate from set pieces this season presents compelling evidence of tactical refinement:

| Metric | Liverpool FC | Premier League Average | Differential |
|---|---|---|---|
| Corners Conversion Rate | 8.4% | 3.2% | +5.2 |
| Free-Kick Goals (per 100 free-kicks) | 2.8 | 1.4 | +1.4 |
| Expected Goals from Set Pieces | 14.7 | 18.2 | -3.5 |
| Actual Set-Piece Goals | 22 | 16.1 | +5.9 |

This data demonstrates that whilst Liverpool generates fewer expected goals from set plays than the league average, their *actual* conversion significantly exceeds projections. This overperformance—a positive difference of 5.9 goals—indicates either exceptional clinical finishing or, more likely, superior coaching in set-piece execution and movement patterns.

## Arne Slot's Tactical Fingerprint

Under Arne Slot's management, set-piece preparation has become noticeably more systematic. The manager's background in Dutch football, where tactical precision is paramount, has clearly influenced Liverpool's approach. Pre-match statistics suggest the Reds spend approximately 25% of training time on set-piece scenarios—an increase from previous seasons.

Ryan Gravenberch and Alexis Mac Allister, both midfielders utilised in deeper positions during set-piece sequences, provide additional attacking outlets. Their positioning data shows they are frequently stationed in dangerous areas during corner routines, offering passing options rather than purely shooting opportunities.

## Defensive Set-Piece Statistics

Whilst offensive set pieces have flourished, Liverpool's defensive vulnerability from opponent set plays warrants mention. The Reds have conceded 6 goals from set pieces this season—above the league average of 4.8. This suggests that whilst Virgil van Dijk and Ibrahima Konate provide strong central defensive presence, covering play from attackers positioned wide during opposition set plays remains inconsistent.

Alisson Becker's distribution statistics show he initiates attacking sequences from goal-kicks with 68% accuracy, well above the Premier League average of 52%, further illustrating Slot's emphasis on controlling momentum from all situations.

## Conclusion

Liverpool's 2025-26 set-piece statistics reveal a team operating at peak efficiency in a previously underutilised attacking dimension. With 22 goals from dead-ball situations—representing 50% of the club's total—Arne Slot has successfully embedded a tactical approach that transforms set pieces into high-percentage scoring opportunities. As the season progresses, maintaining this efficiency whilst reducing defensive vulnerabilities will prove crucial to the Reds' title credentials.
