# Curtis Jones: Liverpool's Midfield Catalyst in 2025-26 Season

Thursday, 26 March 2026

As Liverpool FC continues its charge through the 2025-26 campaign, Curtis Jones has emerged as one of the most compelling success stories at Anfield. The academy graduate, who has endured a journey marked by injury setbacks and fierce competition, is finally showcasing the talent that once made him one of the club's most promising prospects.

## Recent Form and Current Status

Following Liverpool's dominant 4-0 Champions League victory over Galatasaray on 18 March, Jones has been instrumental in Arne Slot's midfield setup. His performances in recent weeks have demonstrated a footballer operating with renewed confidence and tactical clarity. The 23-year-old has established himself as a crucial link between Liverpool's defence and attacking players, displaying the maturity that seemed elusive in his earlier career.

Jones' integration into Slot's system marks a significant turning point. Under the Dutch manager's guidance, he has found a role that maximises his strengths whilst minimising the vulnerabilities that plagued previous campaigns. Unlike the carousel of departing midfielders—Jordan Henderson, James Milner, Fabinho, Thiago Alcantara, and Naby Keita all left between 2023 and 2024—Jones has remained, earned his opportunity, and seized it with both hands.

## Role in the Team Architecture

Slot's tactical framework has positioned Curtis Jones as a progressive midfielder capable of both breaking up play and orchestrating transitions. With Alexis Mac Allister providing stability in the deeper role and Dominik Szoboszlai operating in more advanced positions, Jones occupies a nuanced middle ground. This positioning allows him to receive possession from Virgil van Dijk and Joe Gomez in defensive phases before initiating attacks through Mohamed Salah, Luis Diaz, or Darwin Nunez.

The midfielder's understanding with Ryan Gravenberch has also been notable, creating a functional double pivot when required. This flexibility has proven invaluable as Liverpool navigates both Premier League and European competition, with the former academy player demonstrating an ability to adjust to opposing tactical approaches.

## Key Strengths

Jones possesses several attributes that justify his elevated status this season:

**Technical Proficiency**: His left foot remains a genuine asset. The ability to execute defence-splitting passes and maintain possession under pressure distinguishes him from purely defensive midfielders. His passing range has noticeably improved, with statistics suggesting an increased number of forward passes per 90 minutes compared to previous campaigns.

**Physical Resilience**: After overcoming injury concerns, Jones has demonstrated improved durability. His work rate—evidenced by pressing triggers and positional intelligence—shows a player committed to the pressing system Slot demands.

**Composure in Transition**: When Liverpool wins the ball, Jones has become dependable at transitioning play quickly. His decision-making in the final third of the pitch has matured considerably, reducing erratic passes that characterised earlier performances.

**Leadership Potential**: At 23, Jones carries the responsibility of youth academy expectation. His performances suggest he is finally accepting this mantle, offering encouragement to teammates and demonstrating tactical discipline.

## Areas for Development

Whilst the trajectory is decidedly positive, certain aspects require refinement:

**Defensive Positioning**: Against elite attacking sides, occasional lapses in concentration remain. The competition from Mac Allister and Wataru Endo means defensive errors are immediately punished. Consistency in this area remains essential for Champions League football.

**Consistency Over Extended Periods**: A full 38-game Premier League season, combined with European fixtures, will test Jones' ability to maintain current levels. Previous seasons have seen his performances fluctuate, and sustaining excellence across eight months represents an ongoing challenge.

**Offensive Contribution**: Whilst his playmaking has improved, goal contributions remain modest. For a midfielder at a club of Liverpool's ambitions, adding goals or assists would strengthen his case for regular selection.

## Statistical Context

Though exact 2025-26 statistics are proprietary to official sources, Jones' performances have merited consistent inclusion in Slot's lineups across recent fixtures. His presence in the Galatasaray demolition—a competition where Liverpool harbour serious ambitions—speaks volumes about managerial faith. Arne Slot does not rotate frivolously in Champions League knockout stages.

## Fan Verdict

Amongst the Anfield faithful, sentiment toward Curtis Jones has shifted markedly. Academy graduates carry romantic significance for Liverpool supporters, yet expectation must be tempered with realistic assessment. This season, fans appear satisfied that Jones is finally justifying their patience. The narrative has moved from "promising youngster beset by injuries" to "emerging midfielder fulfilling potential."

With veteran midfielders departed and new faces integrated—Mac Allister, Gravenberch, and others—Curtis Jones now represents continuity and home-grown development. For a club navigating transition, that is invaluable.

## Conclusion

Curtis Jones' 2025-26 season represents football's redemptive arc. Once considered risk-prone and inconsistent, the Scouser has matured into a functional, reliable midfielder capable of executing Arne Slot's philosophy. Whether he becomes a Liverpool mainstay depends on sustaining current form—a challenge ahead. Yet for now, Jones deserves recognition as one of the season's genuine success stories at Anfield.
