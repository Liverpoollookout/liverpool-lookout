# Liverpool's Summer Transfer Crossroads: Why Replacing Salah Must Be Priority One

The elephant in Anfield's room has become impossible to ignore. As Liverpool prepare for summer 2026, whispers about Mohamed Salah's future have transformed into something altogether more ominous. According to reports, the Egyptian winger may depart at season's end—and if that materialises, it demands a fundamental reimagining of Arne Slot's transfer strategy.

Let's be brutally honest: Liverpool cannot simply replace Salah like changing a tyre. Since arriving in 2017, he has redefined what a winger can contribute to elite football. Yet the club's recent departures—Trent Alexander-Arnold's move to Real Madrid, the losses of Thiago Alcantara, Joel Matip, and Roberto Firmino—have already tested Slot's ability to regenerate this squad. Losing Salah would represent an entirely different level of challenge.

## The Case for Prioritising Salah Replacement

If Salah does leave, Liverpool's entire attacking structure implodes. His 20+ goals per season, his ability to drift across the front three, and his relentless intensity in both phases of play are irreplaceable with a single signing. Slot has built this Liverpool side around specific principles—pressing intensity, positional flexibility, penetrative running. Salah embodies all three.

Consider the current attacking personnel. Darwin Nunez offers physicality and directness but not Salah's consistency. Luis Diaz brings pace and directness, yet he's more of a traditional winger. Federico Chiesa provides elegance but has proven vulnerable to injury. Cody Gakpo and Dominik Szoboszlai are valuable, but neither possess Salah's goal-scoring certainty. Curtis Jones shows promise, but promising young talent is precisely what Liverpool cannot afford to gamble on if they lose their talisman.

The reality is stark: without Salah, Liverpool need to recruit an attacker of genuinely world-class calibre immediately. This isn't squad building—this is emergency reconstruction. Every other transfer target, however important, becomes secondary.

## The Counter-Argument: A Balanced Approach

Yet there's a credible counter-view deserving serious consideration. Liverpool's defence, whilst anchored by the magnificent Virgil van Dijk, has vulnerabilities that cannot be ignored. Joe Gomez, Ibrahima Konate, Jarell Quansah, and the promising Conor Bradley represent quality, but depth concerns persist. Caoimhin Kelleher has proven himself capable, but relying on a deputy goalkeeper in key competitions carries risk.

Moreover, the midfield, despite featuring Harvey Elliott, Ryan Gravenberch, Alexis Mac Allister, and Wataru Endo, could benefit from additional creative reinforcement. If Salah stays—and let's remember, nothing is confirmed—then Liverpool might argue that addressing defensive solidity and midfield versatility represents better value allocation.

There's also the philosophical argument: building exceptional teams isn't about one player. Manchester City's success stems from systematic excellence, not individual brilliance. Should Liverpool not adopt a similar mentality, spreading resources across the pitch rather than concentrating investment in a Salah replacement?

## The Verdict: Accept Reality and Act

Here's the uncomfortable truth: Liverpool must prepare for both scenarios, but planning must assume Salah's departure is possible.

Slot inherited a squad mid-transition. The departures of Henderson, Fabinho, Milner, Firmino, and Thiago represented a generational shift. Slot has stabilised this remarkably well—the 4-0 demolition of Galatasaray at Anfield in the Champions League Round of 16 proves this team can compete at the highest level. But that victory also highlighted a vulnerability: Liverpool need goals from multiple sources because relying solely on Salah creates tactical rigidity.

My stance is this: prioritise a world-class attacking talent capable of playing across the front three—a player offering Salah's goal-scoring threat and positional intelligence. This might be a proven 26-27-year-old in their peak, not a speculative young talent. Simultaneously, reinforce the back four and consider midfield depth as secondary priorities.

The departures of Alexander-Arnold, Matip, and others proved Liverpool can transition successfully. But transitions require exceptional leadership and planning. Arne Slot must be ruthlessly clear-eyed about Salah's situation and act decisively if confirmation comes.

Anfield has weathered storms before. But this summer will define whether this Liverpool side remains competitive at Europe's elite level or begins a longer decline. The stakes could not be higher.
