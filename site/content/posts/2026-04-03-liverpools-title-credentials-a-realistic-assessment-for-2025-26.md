# Liverpool's Title Credentials: A Realistic Assessment for 2025-26

Liverpool's devastating 4-0 demolition of Galatasaray at Anfield in the Champions League Round of 16 would have us believe Arne Slot's side are unstoppable. But can they truly mount a credible Premier League title challenge? The answer is more nuanced than the scoreline suggests.

## The Case for Optimism

There's genuine reason to believe Liverpool can compete at the highest level this season. Mohamed Salah remains in the form of his life—his journey at Anfield continues to produce moments of clinical brilliance that define campaigns. Virgil van Dijk's leadership at the back, combined with the defensive solidity of Ibrahima Konate and Joe Gomez, provides the foundation every title-winning team requires. 

Darwin Nunez's intensity, Luis Diaz's unpredictability, and the midfield presence of Alexis Mac Allister and Dominik Szoboszlai give Slot genuine attacking options. The 4-0 dismantling of Galatasaray showcased a side operating with precision and purpose. When Liverpool click, few teams in Europe can match their intensity.

Moreover, the departure of depth issues has been addressed through shrewd additions. Federico Chiesa's arrival adds European pedigree on the wing, whilst Caoimhin Kelleher provides genuine quality as backup to Alisson Becker. Ryan Gravenberch has matured into a crucial midfielder, offering balance between defence and attack. These aren't panic buys—they're considered investments.

## The Uncomfortable Truth

Yet this is where realism must prevail. Losing Trent Alexander-Arnold to Real Madrid in summer 2024 wasn't merely the departure of a full-back; it represented the erosion of institutional knowledge. Yes, Conor Bradley and Konstantinos Tsimikas can deputise, but there's no replacing a player of Trent's calibre.

The midfield rebuild remains incomplete. While Mac Allister and Szoboszlai are excellent, the departures of Jordan Henderson, Thiago Alcantara, and Fabinho—players who understood the Liverpool way—have left scars. Wataru Endo and Ryan Gravenberch are quality operators, but they're still establishing themselves at Anfield's intensity level.

Then there's the elephant in the room: Mohamed Salah. The speculation surrounding his future, with reports suggesting he could depart at season's end, creates uncertainty. Regardless of what happens, relying so heavily on a single player—however brilliant—is precarious for a title challenge. Curtis Jones and Harvey Elliott must step up, but neither has consistently demonstrated they can be consistent Premier League winners.

## The Manchester Problem

Let's be brutally honest: Manchester City and Manchester United have strengthened considerably. The competition for the title is fiercer than it's been in years. Under Slot, Liverpool are undoubtedly improving—the Galatasaray performance proved that. But improvement doesn't automatically translate to titles, particularly when competitors are equally ambitious.

## The Verdict

Liverpool are genuine contenders, not pretenders. With Salah in the side, Van Dijk marshalling the defence, and Slot's tactical acumen developing the squad, they'll certainly challenge. But winning the title? That requires not just a brilliant run of form (the 4-0 win over Galatasaray shows they're capable), but consistency across 38 matches and fortunate injury luck.

My assessment: Liverpool will finish in the top four and likely top two, competing for silverware in the Champions League. But if you're backing them for the Premier League title, you should do so with eyes wide open. They're contenders in a competitive field, not favourites. And in football, that's often the difference between glory and heartbreak.

Anfield will roar again this season. Whether it roars loudest in May remains genuinely uncertain.
