# Ryan Gravenberch: The Midfield Anchor Proving His Worth Under Slot

When Arne Slot took the reins at Liverpool, many questioned whether the club's midfield possessed the necessary dynamism to compete across four competitions. Five months into the 2025-26 campaign, one player has emerged as a quiet architect of their success: Ryan Gravenberch.

The Dutch midfielder, who arrived at Anfield with considerable pedigree but inconsistent form during his opening months on Merseyside, has quietly become one of the most essential cogs in Slot's machine. His display during Liverpool's commanding 4-0 demolition of Galatasaray in the Champions League Round of 16 epitomised the evolution of his contribution this season.

## From Prospect to Protagonist

Gravenberch's trajectory at Liverpool has been unconventional. Drafted in from Bayern Munich with undeniable technical credentials, he spent his first campaign searching for consistency and a defined role. Under Slot's methodical approach, however, the 22-year-old has discovered a clarity of purpose that had previously eluded him.

The current manager's preference for controlling possession through intelligent midfield positioning has suited Gravenberch perfectly. Rather than asking him to cover excessive ground defensively or drive forward recklessly, Slot has tasked him with being the quarterback of Liverpool's build-up play—a role that maximises his strengths whilst minimising his weaknesses.

## The Technical Excellence

What stands out most about Gravenberch's season is his composure in possession. Operating alongside Alexis Mac Allister and Dominik Szoboszlai, he has averaged impressive passing completion rates whilst successfully implementing Slot's possession-based philosophy. His ability to receive the ball under pressure and execute a quick, intelligent pass has been invaluable in bypassing opposition pressing.

His press resistance—often underrated in modern football discourse—has proven particularly valuable when Liverpool face high-intensity pressing teams. Where another midfielder might panic or rush his decision-making, Gravenberch's calm demeanour allows the team to maintain their shape and transition periods.

The Galatasaray performance showcased this perfectly. As Liverpool swept aside their Turkish visitors with clinical efficiency, Gravenberch controlled the tempo, dictating play with the authority of a midfielder far more experienced in the Champions League's grand stages.

## Areas for Development

Yet no analysis of Gravenberch's season would be complete without acknowledging where improvement remains necessary. His defensive contribution, whilst functional, doesn't quite match the elite standards set by some Premier League midfield pivots. He relies heavily on the cover provided by his teammates rather than suffocating opponents through aggressive pressing.

Additionally, his goal-scoring threat remains virtually non-existent. A midfielder operating in Liverpool's system should harbour aspirations of contributing to the attacking third more regularly, yet Gravenberch has failed to translate his technical excellence into decisive moments in the final third.

His physical presence—occasionally exposed against more robust midfield operators—also warrants development as he prepares for the intensifying demands of English football's closing months.

## Fan Perspective and Statistical Context

Amongst the Liverpool faithful, Gravenberch's reputation has undergone a notable rehabilitation. Social media sentiment, often harsh towards the midfielder during his early Anfield struggles, has shifted markedly in his favour. Supporters now recognise that his contribution—whilst perhaps less visually spectacular than a Szoboszlai or Mac Allister—remains fundamentally important to the collective enterprise.

Statistically, his performances across the season validate this perception. Possession retention figures remain strong, whilst his distribution metrics suggest someone operating at a respectable level for a midfielder of his tender years in a hyperactive league.

## The Road Ahead

As Liverpool progress deeper into European competition and mount their domestic assault, Gravenberch's continued development remains crucial. Should he add greater defensive aggression and occasional attacking threat to his already impressive technical base, he could evolve from capable midfielder into something genuinely special.

Under Slot's tutelage, the trajectory is unquestionably positive. Whether that culminates in the kind of consistency and excellence demanded of Liverpool's midfield remains the intriguing subplot of the season ahead.

For now, Gravenberch has become an increasingly difficult player to displace—a remarkable turnaround for someone who, not long ago, appeared destined for an uncertain future at Anfield.
