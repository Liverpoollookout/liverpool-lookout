# Can Liverpool Still Challenge for the Premier League? A Realistic Assessment of Our Title Credentials

Watching Liverpool stumble through recent weeks—a humbling FA Cup exit, that damaging defeat to Manchester City where Erling Haaland ran riot—it's tempting to conclude that our title credentials have evaporated entirely. Yet such knee-jerk conclusions ignore both our undeniable quality and the very real obstacles standing between Anfield and another Premier League crown.

The narrative has shifted dramatically since Arne Slot's arrival. For months, we dared to dream of challenging City's dominance. Our emphatic 4-0 demolition of Galatasaray at Anfield in the Champions League showed exactly what we're capable of when firing on all cylinders. Mohamed Salah was imperious. The defensive solidity offered by Virgil van Dijk and Ibrahima Konate was reassuring. The midfield cohesion between Dominik Szoboszlai, Ryan Gravenberch, and Alexis Mac Allister hummed with rhythm. On nights like that, we genuinely looked like champions-in-waiting.

But therein lies the fundamental problem: we've proven we *can* dominate, yet we've failed to do so consistently. That's not a title-winning formula. Manchester City thrives on relentless excellence. Chelsea, under their various recent regimes, have shown similar ruthlessness. Liverpool, by contrast, remains frustratingly inconsistent.

Consider the departed talents. We lost Trent Alexander-Arnold to Real Madrid—a world-class right-back who was irreplaceable at his peak. Before him, the summer departures of Joel Matip and Thiago Alcantara meant we'd surrendered genuine experience and technical excellence. Yes, Conor Bradley and Alexis Mac Allister have emerged as capable replacements, but there's a qualitative difference when you're replacing world-beaters with young prospects. We're rebuilding whilst simultaneously expecting to compete for silverware.

**The counter-argument is compelling, though.** We still possess a genuinely elite squad. Salah remains one of Europe's finest right-wingers. Van Dijk, despite being in his thirties, continues to dominate aerially and positionally. Darwin Nunez, when confident, offers the goal-threat we desperately need. Luis Diaz has flourished in a Liverpool shirt. Alisson Becker remains one of the Premier League's best goalkeepers. Slot himself is an accomplished tactician—his reign has already produced moments of genuine brilliance.

Moreover, the domestic cup setbacks, whilst disappointing, shouldn't entirely taint our league credentials. We've competed fiercely in the Premier League despite those distractions. The fact that we were genuine contenders for much of the season suggests the foundations are sound. One or two injuries to key players—particularly van Dijk or Salah—and our season evaporates, but that's true of any side.

Here's my verdict: **Liverpool are legitimate top-four contenders, but genuine title challengers? Not yet.** We lack the relentless consistency that separates champions from the merely excellent. Our vulnerability to elite opposition—that City display was catastrophic—exposes defensive and midfield limitations that Slot hasn't yet solved. We've proven we can beat anyone on our day, but we've equally proven we can lose to anyone when circumstances align against us.

The pathway forward isn't mysterious. We need either a sustained run of 15+ consecutive victories without defeat, which would require injury-free fortune and impossible levels of performance, or we need genuine squad reinforcements this summer. Slot's system demands midfield control and defensive solidity. Currently, we offer flashes of both, never quite the sustained excellence required.

I remain a believer in what Slot can build. This isn't a crisis; it's an adjustment period. But let's be honest with ourselves: unless something changes dramatically between now and season's end, or unless we mount an improbable winning streak, we're looking at a top-four finish rather than a title challenge. That's respectable, but it's not what Liverpool supporters crave.

The ingredients for dominance remain at Anfield. Whether Slot can mix them into a title-winning formula? That question remains agonisingly unanswered.
