# Liverpool Face Summer Upheaval: Salah Departure Could Spark Major Anfield Reshuffle

Liverpool FC are bracing for significant squad changes this summer, with Mohamed Salah's anticipated departure from Anfield creating a considerable void that will dominate transfer planning under manager Arne Slot.

## Salah Exit Confirmed for End of Season

According to recent reports, Mohamed Salah is set to leave Liverpool at the conclusion of the 2024-25 campaign, marking the end of an era for the Egyptian winger. Salah's departure represents one of the most significant exits since the departures of established figures like Roberto Firmino, James Milner, and Jordan Henderson in 2023.

The loss of Salah, who has been instrumental to Liverpool's attacking prowess, will necessitate a comprehensive recruitment strategy from Slot and the club's sporting director. His departure follows the blueprint of recent seasons, where the club have been forced to rebuild following the exit of key personnel.

## Defensive Reinforcements Required

Beyond Salah's exit, sources suggest Liverpool may face additional departures in defence. Whilst Virgil van Dijk remains contracted and committed to Anfield, questions persist regarding the squad's depth at centre-back. Current options include Ibrahima Konate, Joe Gomez, and academy prospect Jarell Quansah, but Slot is likely to seek experienced reinforcements to challenge for the Premier League title.

The right-back position, vacated by Trent Alexander-Arnold's summer 2024 transfer to Real Madrid, remains a priority area. Conor Bradley and Konstantinos Tsimikas provide options on the flank, but Liverpool may pursue an established alternative to strengthen this critical area.

## Attack Requires Major Overhaul

With Salah's imminent departure, Liverpool's attacking force must be restructured immediately. Current options including Darwin Nunez, Luis Diaz, Cody Gakpo, Federico Chiesa, and Diogo Jota will shoulder increased responsibility, but the club cannot rely solely on this quartet to deliver Champions League and Premier League credentials.

According to reports, Liverpool are actively scouting replacements in elite leagues across Europe. Potential targets are understood to be operating at similar calibre to Salah, though specific names remain undisclosed at this stage.

## Midfield Stability Amid Uncertainty

The midfield retains greater stability compared to attacking and defensive departments. Dominik Szoboszlai, Ryan Gravenberch, Alexis Mac Allister, and Curtis Jones represent a solid foundation, with Wataru Endo providing experience. However, sources suggest Slot may seek an additional midfielder to enhance creativity and control following departures during the previous off-season, including Thiago Alcantara and Fabinho in 2023.

## Goalkeeping Succession Clear

At least one area of certainty exists: goalkeeping. Alisson Becker remains Liverpool's undisputed number one, with Caoimhin Kelleher providing capable backup. The Irish stopper's performances have warranted recognition, eliminating any requirement for goalkeeping recruitment this summer.

## Transfer Window Timeline

With the 2025-26 season approaching, Liverpool's transfer window will be critical. The club must replace Salah's goal-scoring threat, strengthen defensive options, and potentially add creative midfield reinforcement. Arne Slot will oversee what represents the most significant summer transfer activity since his appointment, fundamentally reshaping the squad's character.

## Conclusion

Liverpool's summer transfer campaign will define the club's trajectory for seasons ahead. Whilst departures of established players—echoing losses of Henderson, Firmino, and Fabinho previously—present challenges, they also provide opportunity for Slot to implement his vision comprehensively at Anfield. The coming weeks will prove decisive in determining whether Liverpool strengthen sufficiently to maintain their competitive edge across domestic and European competitions.
