# Liverpool Face Uncertain Summer as Salah's Future Dominates Transfer Agenda

Liverpool FC finds itself at a crossroads as the 2025-26 season approaches, with manager **Arne Slot** preparing for a significant squad overhaul following months of uncertainty surrounding key personnel.

The most pressing concern remains **Mohamed Salah's** impending departure, with the Egyptian winger widely expected to leave Anfield when his contract expires at the end of this season. Sources suggest Salah's exit will reshape Liverpool's attacking options considerably, marking the end of an era that has defined the club's success over the past six years.

## Salah's Departure Creates Vacancy

Salah's imminent exit from Anfield leaves Arne Slot with a significant challenge on the right flank. The 32-year-old has been instrumental to Liverpool's attacking prowess, and his replacement will need to fill considerable boots. According to reports, the club is considering several attacking profiles to bolster their forward line alongside **Luis Diaz**, **Darwin Nunez**, **Cody Gakpo**, and **Diogo Jota**.

The loss of such an experienced presence also impacts Liverpool's dressing-room leadership, particularly as **Virgil van Dijk** and **Alisson Becker** continue their own contract negotiations at the club.

## Contract Situations Uncertain

Beyond Salah, Liverpool faces uncertainty regarding several key defenders and midfielders. **Van Dijk's** contract status remains a focal point for supporters and journalists alike, though negotiations are understood to be progressing. Meanwhile, **Alisson Becker's** long-term future also requires clarification as Slot builds his vision for future campaigns.

In midfield, **Dominik Szoboszlai**, **Ryan Gravenberch**, **Alexis Mac Allister**, and **Curtis Jones** represent Liverpool's creative spine, yet their futures deserve clarity before the summer window opens.

## Potential Outgoings Beyond Salah

According to reports, Liverpool may consider additional departures to fund squad improvements. **Federico Chiesa**, who arrived on loan with an option to buy, represents a decision point for the club. Sources suggest Slot will evaluate whether the Italian winger justifies a permanent investment or whether resources should be redirected elsewhere.

**Jarell Quansah**, the young defender, could attract interest from elsewhere despite being viewed as a prospect for Liverpool's future. It is claimed several Premier League clubs monitor his development, though Slot values his potential alongside **Ibrahima Konate** and **Joe Gomez** in the defensive line.

## Academy Players in Spotlight

Young talents including **Harvey Elliott** and **Conor Bradley** must prove their worth under Slot's scrutiny. These homegrown players represent Liverpool's pathway to sustained success, yet competition from experienced signings may limit their opportunities.

## Slot's Summer Blueprint

Arne Slot has demonstrated clear tactical preferences during his initial months at Anfield. The Dutchman favours a compact defensive shape, demanding midfield runners, and clinical finishing—attributes that will guide his recruitment strategy. With figures like **Van Dijk**, **Alisson**, **Szoboszlai**, and **Mac Allister** as his core, the manager must identify complementary players to replace departing personnel.

Reports suggest Liverpool will target proven performers rather than speculative signings, particularly given the financial implications of Salah's departure. The club typically recycles revenue from major sales back into the squad, ensuring competitive depth.

## Looking Ahead

The 2025-26 season represents a transitional period for Liverpool FC. Whilst the departures of established figures like Salah create uncertainty, they also present opportunities for fresh tactical approaches under Slot's guidance. The manager has successfully integrated new players during his tenure, evidenced by the contributions of **Gravenberch**, **Chiesa**, and others.

As transfer activity accelerates through spring and into summer, Liverpool supporters must prepare for change. The club's recruitment department faces a crucial window to balance experience with emerging talent, ensuring Anfield remains a competitive force in the Premier League and Europe.

The coming months will define Liverpool's trajectory for years to come.
