# Liverpool's Title Credentials: A Realistic Assessment for the 2025-26 Season

Thursday evening at Anfield this week told us everything we need to know about Liverpool's current standing. A commanding 4-0 Champions League demolition of Galatasaray demonstrated the ruthless efficiency that Arne Slot has cultivated at Merseyside. Yet as we assess whether Liverpool can genuinely challenge for the Premier League title this season, one uncomfortable truth persists: this squad, for all its quality, faces more headwinds than tailwinds.

## The Case for Title Contention

Let's not dismiss Liverpool's credentials entirely. Virgil van Dijk remains an imperious defensive presence, anchoring a back line that, when fit, rivals any in European football. The arrival of Federico Chiesa added fresh attacking impetus, whilst the creative brilliance of Dominik Szoboszlai continues to orchestrate Liverpool's midfield with increasing maturity.

Mohamed Salah, despite the ongoing uncertainty surrounding his contract situation, remains a world-class operator capable of winning matches single-handedly. Darwin Nunez has matured considerably, and the midfield pairing of Ryan Gravenberch and Alexis Mac Allister provides genuine steel and technical security. Luis Diaz's pace and verve on the flank remains a genuine asset.

Under Slot's measured tactical guidance, Liverpool has demonstrated consistency. That 4-0 thrashing of Galatasaray wasn't a one-off—it reflected a team executing a clear blueprint with precision. The manager's continental approach has brought structure and coherence to a side that occasionally looked disjointed under his predecessor.

## The Counter-Argument: Structural Concerns

However, the departures of summer 2024 cannot be understated. Trent Alexander-Arnold's move to Real Madrid represented far more than losing a full-back; we lost our most consistently creative defensive contributor. Joel Matip, Thiago Alcantara, and the summer 2023 exits of Fabinho and Roberto Firmino stripped away institutional knowledge and experience.

The right-back situation now relies on Conor Bradley, a talented youngster, and Joe Gomez in unfamiliar territory. Whilst serviceable, neither offers the creative spark or defensive assurance that Trent provided season after season. Midfield depth concerns persist despite the additions—Wataru Endo and Curtis Jones are competent, but Liverpool lacks the elite-tier cover that separates title contenders from challengers.

More pressingly, there's the elephant in the room: Mohamed Salah's contract situation. According to recent reports, uncertainty surrounds his future beyond this season. Should Liverpool lose their most prolific attacking threat, the title conversation becomes purely academic. A player capable of 20+ goals annually isn't easily replaced, regardless of squad depth.

Competition from Manchester City—despite their current struggles—remains formidable. Arsenal have built remarkable consistency. Even Chelsea, with their investment, cannot be discounted. Can Liverpool realistically outscore these rivals over 38 matches?

## The Verdict: Genuine Contenders, But Not Favourites

Liverpool sits in that uncomfortable middle ground: too good to dismiss, yet too fragile to back as title favourites. Slot has implemented a coherent system, and performances like the Galatasaray victory showcase what this side is capable of executing. Alisson Becker's shot-stopping continues to provide insurance; Ibrahima Konate's renaissance has proven invaluable alongside Van Dijk.

Yet realistic assessment demands acknowledging the constraints. The loss of Trent cannot be chemically replaced through tactical adjustment alone. Youth integration—Harvey Elliott, Jarell Quansah, and Bradley—is admirable but inherently inconsistent over a season's length. One serious injury to key personnel exposes depth issues markedly.

My verdict? Liverpool are genuine title contenders who could absolutely win the Premier League—but they're not the most likely outcome. They're third or fourth favourites at best, capable of pushing City or Arsenal hard but more realistically fighting for Champions League qualification and domestic cup success.

Slot's methodical approach provides foundation. The midfield machinery hums with purpose. Defence, when intact, commands respect. But those departures from 2023-24 created gaps that, whilst papered over competently, remain identifiable. Until Salah's future is clarified and that right-back position offers greater creative assurance, Liverpool's title credentials remain intriguing rather than commanding.

Anfield's fortress status remains genuine. But winning titles in May requires more than impressive midweek European performances. It demands sustained excellence, injury fortune, and a squad with genuine depth at every level. Liverpool possesses the first and perhaps the second. The jury remains decidedly out on the third.
