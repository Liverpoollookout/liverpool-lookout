# Liverpool Face Uncertain Summer: Contract Situations & Potential Departures Loom

Liverpool FC's 2025-26 campaign has been marked by frustration both on and off the pitch, with mounting pressure on manager Arne Slot following the Reds' recent FA Cup capitulation. As the transfer window approaches, focus intensifies on several key contract situations and potential outgoings from Anfield that could reshape the club's squad architecture.

## Contract Uncertainty at the Summit

The most pressing concern centres on **Mohamed Salah**, whose contract status remains a topic of intense speculation. According to reports, discussions between Salah's representatives and Liverpool's hierarchy have stalled, creating uncertainty over the Egyptian winger's long-term future. The 33-year-old remains pivotal to Arne Slot's attacking setup, yet sources suggest the club faces a critical decision regarding fresh terms.

Similarly, **Virgil van Dijk's** position demands immediate clarification. The captain's recent public frustration regarding the squad's mentality—following the cup exit—has intensified questions about whether Liverpool's defensive colossus might seek a new challenge. Though van Dijk has committed to Anfield previously, his current contract situation requires resolution to maintain stability at the back.

## Potential Summer Departures

Beyond headline names, several squad players face uncertain futures. **Caoimhin Kelleher**, the accomplished backup goalkeeper to **Alisson Becker**, continues attracting interest from clubs seeking established Premier League experience. Reports suggest multiple sides have registered their interest, though Liverpool are unlikely to facilitate any exit for their capable stopper without securing a replacement.

**Joe Gomez** represents another potential outgoing. The versatile defender, capable of operating across the backline, is claimed to be a target for several top-flight clubs seeking defensive reinforcement. His accumulated experience and adaptability make him an attractive prospect, though any departure would thin Liverpool's defensive resources significantly.

## Fringe Squad Options

Conor Bradley**, the young right-back, remains subject to speculation regarding loan opportunities or permanent moves. Sources suggest several Championship and lower Premier League sides have enquired about the prospect, though Slot may view him as valuable squad depth following **Trent Alexander-Arnold's** departure to Real Madrid last summer.

**Wataru Endo** and **Konstantinos Tsimikas** occupy interesting positions within the squad hierarchy. The Japanese midfielder has struggled for consistent minutes under Slot's regime, whilst the Greek left-back faces stiff competition from **Luis Diaz's** occasional deployment on the flank. According to reports, both players could attract suitors seeking Premier League experience at reduced rates.

## Summer Recruitment Strategy

Slot's summer priorities appear focused on reinforcing attacking depth and defensive cover. **Darwin Nunez**, **Luis Diaz**, and **Cody Gakpo** form an exciting attacking triumvirate, yet injuries to any key player expose gaps. The potential exits of fringe figures may generate funds for targeted additions.

**Dominik Szoboszlai**, **Ryan Gravenberch**, and **Alexis Mac Allister** provide midfield backbone, yet midfield depth remains a concern should injuries disrupt continuity. Reports suggest Slot has identified targets to bolster this area, though specific links remain unconfirmed.

## The Road Ahead

With Liverpool enduring disappointing cup exits and mounting pressure on Slot, the summer transfer window becomes crucial for squad resetting. The club's hierarchy must navigate delicate conversations regarding Salah and van Dijk whilst simultaneously balancing outgoings with strategic acquisitions.

Clearly, 2026's summer window represents a pivotal moment for Liverpool. How the club manages contract negotiations, potential departures, and incoming recruitment will significantly influence whether Anfield's campaign recovers the trajectory fans demand. Arne Slot's ability to balance these complexities could determine his long-term success at Anfield.
