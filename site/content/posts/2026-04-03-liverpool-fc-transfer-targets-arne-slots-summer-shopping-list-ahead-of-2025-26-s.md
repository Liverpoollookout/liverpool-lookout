# Liverpool FC Transfer Targets: Arne Slot's Summer Shopping List Ahead of 2025-26 Season

As Liverpool FC looks ahead to the 2025-26 campaign, manager Arne Slot is reportedly preparing an ambitious transfer strategy to bolster Anfield's squad. With several departures in recent years—including Trent Alexander-Arnold's move to Real Madrid last summer—the Reds are eyeing strategic reinforcements across multiple positions.

## Right-Back Succession Planning

Following Trent Alexander-Arnold's exit to the Spanish capital, Liverpool must address the right-back position comprehensively. Conor Bradley has shown promise, but Slot is believed to be exploring options to provide proven competition and depth. According to reports, several European clubs' defenders have been monitored, though no concrete offers have materialised at present.

## Midfield Reinforcement

While Dominik Szoboszlai, Ryan Gravenberch, and Alexis Mac Allister have impressed under Slot's management, Liverpool sources suggest the club remains keen to add another creative midfielder. The departure of Thiago Alcantara in 2024 created a quality void in the engine room. Reports indicate Slot's scouts have compiled a shortlist of targets who could add technical excellence and European pedigree—players capable of operating in the manager's favoured pressing system.

Curtis Jones and Harvey Elliott continue developing, but additional experience at the highest level could accelerate Liverpool's title ambitions. According to credible reports, potential fee estimates for top targets in this category range from £40-60 million.

## Attacking Depth

With Mohamed Salah, Darwin Nunez, Luis Diaz, Cody Gakpo, and Diogo Jota currently spearheading Liverpool's offensive threat, Slot possesses considerable attacking firepower. However, sources suggest the club monitors the market for potential backup options. Federico Chiesa's injury record means rotation options remain strategically important.

Anfield's attacking trio of Salah, Nunez, and Diaz represents world-class potential, yet depth recruitment—particularly for rotation in domestic cups—remains sensible planning.

## Defensive Consolidation

Virgil van Dijk's leadership anchors Liverpool's back line, supported by Ibrahima Konate, Joe Gomez, and Jarell Quansah. The central defensive core appears robust following Joel Matip's departure in 2024. However, it is claimed that Slot may explore left-back options beyond Konstantinos Tsimikas, should emerging opportunities arise in the market.

Alisson Becker remains Liverpool's first-choice goalkeeper, with Caoimhin Kelleher providing quality backup. No changes are anticipated in goal.

## Transfer Window Expectations

Liverpool's recruitment strategy under Slot prioritises sustainable, intelligent spending rather than panic purchases. The club's academy pathway continues producing talent, with emerging prospects potentially challenging for first-team opportunities.

According to transfer specialists, Slot's preference centres on players adaptable to Liverpool's high-pressing philosophy. The manager has demonstrated preference for technically gifted midfielders and dynamic forwards capable of operating across multiple attacking positions.

## Market Reality

It is claimed that Liverpool's summer budget remains healthy but not unlimited. Fee estimates for primary targets suggest investment in the £35-75 million bracket—reflecting the club's ambition whilst maintaining financial prudence.

With the 2025-26 season presenting fresh title opportunities, Arne Slot's transfer activity will prove pivotal. Any summer recruitment must address identified weaknesses without disrupting the cohesion already established among Mohamed Salah, Darwin Nunez, Luis Diaz, and the midfield core of Szoboszlai and Gravenberch.
