# Wataru Endo: The Unsung Midfield Maestro Anchoring Liverpool's Title Push

While Mohamed Salah's brilliance and Darwin Nunez's raw power dominate Liverpool FC headlines, there exists a quieter, equally vital force operating in the engine room at Anfield. Wataru Endo, the Japanese midfielder signed to replace departing midfield stalwarts, has become the invisible architect of Arne Slot's reshaped Liverpool machine in the 2025-26 season.

The loss of pivotal figures—Fabinho, Jordan Henderson, Thiago Alcântara, and James Milner all departing within recent seasons—created a void that demanded both defensive solidity and creative orchestration. Enter Endo, a player whose metronomic control and positional discipline have quietly revolutionised how Liverpool transitions between defence and attack.

## The Perfect Slot Fit

Under Arne Slot's tenure, Endo has flourished as the manager's primary midfield controller. Unlike the more flamboyant approaches of previous regimes, Slot's philosophy demands a midfielder capable of breaking possession chains with intelligent positioning whilst maintaining the team's structural integrity. Endo embodies this precisely.

His partnership with Ryan Gravenberch and Alexis Mac Allister has created a formidable midfield triumvirate, each player complementing the others' strengths. Whilst Gravenberch provides box-to-box energy and Mac Allister contributes Argentine guile, Endo anchors the entire operation with tireless running and exceptional reading of the game.

## Season Statistics and Impact

The 2025-26 campaign has witnessed Endo's most influential spell in a Liverpool shirt. His ball recovery rate remains among the Premier League's elite, whilst his pass completion percentage consistently exceeds 88%, crucial in Slot's possession-dominated system. Against Galatasaray in the Champions League Round of 16, where Liverpool's dominance culminated in a comprehensive 4-0 victory at Anfield, Endo's control in midfield proved foundational—orchestrating transitions that allowed Salah, Luis Diaz, and the attacking triumvirate to flourish.

His positional intelligence prevents the opposition from establishing rhythm against Liverpool's defence, marshalled by the commanding Virgil van Dijk and the increasingly assured Ibrahima Konate. Without Endo's grafting, Van Dijk and company would face significantly greater pressure.

## Strengths That Shine

Endo's greatest asset remains his distribution under pressure. Opposing forwards frequently press Liverpool's deeper areas, yet Endo consistently finds forward passes that penetrate the press rather than sideways escapes. This progressive passing directly facilitates chances for Salah, Cody Gakpo, and Diogo Jota.

Secondly, his work ethic proves unmatched. Rarely does Endo conserve energy; instead, he runs relentlessly, covering ground Slot's system demands. His defensive contributions—tackles, interceptions, and blocks—provide crucial defensive cover, particularly important as Joe Gomez, Jarell Quansah, and Konstantinos Tsimikas rotate through the fullback positions.

Thirdly, his mentality commands respect among teammates. Following the departures of Henderson and Milner, leadership became scarce in Liverpool's midfield. Endo has gradually assumed this responsibility, offering steady guidance without demanding attention.

## Areas for Growth

Yet Endo remains imperfect. His attacking contributions remain minimal—goal contributions remain sparse even as Liverpool dominate matches. Whilst defensive solidity cannot be questioned, Slot might occasionally require Endo to venture forward, adding penetration alongside Mac Allister's more creative ventures.

Additionally, high-intensity pressing occasionally exposes his limited acceleration. Quicker, more mobile opponents occasionally find space in transitions, though Liverpool's compact shape typically compensates.

Furthermore, consistency in crucial matches demands scrutiny. His performances fluctuate, occasionally disappearing against elite opposition, though this reflects individual consistency rather than tactical incompatibility.

## Fan Verdict

Among Anfield's faithful, Endo has evolved from questioned signing to respected specialist. Initial scepticism regarding his technical level has evaporated as supporters recognise his invaluable role. Social media conversations increasingly acknowledge his importance—supporters recognising that matches where Endo controls proceedings typically result in victories.

His understated excellence represents modern football's evolution: the midfielder who wins silverware through intelligence and discipline rather than spectacular performance. In an era where midfield dominance determines championship outcomes, Endo's contribution proves absolutely fundamental.

## Looking Forward

As Liverpool continues its 2025-26 assault on multiple competitions, Wataru Endo remains the quiet heartbeat. Whilst Salah's genius and Van Dijk's defensive mastery attract deserved plaudits, Endo's metronomic excellence ensures Liverpool functions as the well-oiled machine Slot envisioned.

Underestimated, underappreciated, and utterly essential—that is Wataru Endo's Liverpool story. In the noise surrounding Salah's future and the excitement of champions' football, never forget the Japanese midfielder quietly orchestrating Anfield's resurgence.
