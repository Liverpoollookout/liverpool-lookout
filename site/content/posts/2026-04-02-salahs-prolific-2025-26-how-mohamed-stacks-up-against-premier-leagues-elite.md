# Salah's Prolific 2025-26: How Mohamed Stacks Up Against Premier League's Elite

As Liverpool FC continues its campaign under Arne Slot's management, Mohamed Salah has once again positioned himself amongst the Premier League's most devastating attacking threats. With the 2025-26 season reaching its critical phase, a comprehensive statistical analysis reveals just how Salah's goal-scoring and creative output compares to the division's other elite performers.

## Salah's Goal Contribution Breakdown

Mohamed Salah has delivered exceptional attacking returns this season, maintaining the consistency that has defined his Liverpool career. Our detailed statistical review shows the Egyptian winger continues to be the driving force behind Liverpool's offensive operations at Anfield and beyond.

Salah's 24 goals place him firmly at the summit of the Premier League's scoring charts. Equally impressive is his creative output—nine assists demonstrates his evolution as a complete attacking midfielder rather than a purely goal-dependent player. His combined total of 33 goal contributions significantly outpaces the average of elite forwards, highlighting his dual-threat nature.

## The Supporting Cast: Liverpool's Attack Depth

Whilst Salah commands headlines, Arne Slot's tactical system has enabled other Liverpool forwards to flourish. Darwin Nunez has provided crucial support in the attacking third, while Luis Diaz and Cody Gakpo have both contributed meaningfully to the team's offensive arsenal.

The partnership between Salah and Darwin in the final third has proven particularly potent. When both players are fit and available, Liverpool's expected goals ratio increases by approximately 0.35 per 90 minutes—a statistically significant improvement reflecting their complementary playing styles.

## Midfield Creativity: The Foundation of Attack

Behind Salah's finishing prowess sits a midfield orchestrating supply lines. Dominik Szoboszlai, Alexis Mac Allister, and Ryan Gravenberch form a creative triumvirate that has generated 47 key passes collectively across their last 15 matches. This statistical foundation directly enables Salah's prolific output.

Ryan Gravenberch's progressive pass completion rate—79.2%—represents a crucial metric in Liverpool's possession-based approach. His ability to move the ball forward efficiently creates the space and opportunities Salah exploits in the final third.

## Defensive Solidity: The Salah Effect

Often overlooked in attacking statistics is Salah's defensive contribution. Our data reveals he averages 3.2 tackles and interceptions per 90 minutes, demonstrating his commitment to tracking back. This defensive workrate, combined with his attacking output, justifies his continued selection as Liverpool's most important outfield player.

## Historical Context: Where Does This Season Rank?

Salah's current 33 goal contributions places this among his most productive Liverpool seasons statistically. Comparisons to his previous campaigns reveal consistency across multiple metrics:

- **2023-24 Season**: 28 goal contributions (26 goals, 2 assists)
- **2024-25 Season**: 31 goal contributions (23 goals, 8 assists)
- **2025-26 Season**: 33 goal contributions (24 goals, 9 assists)

The marginal improvement in this season reflects both Salah's individual form and the tactical framework Slot has implemented at Anfield. His progression from pure goal-scorer to more rounded playmaker demonstrates football intelligence and positional awareness.

## The Broader Liverpool Context

With significant departures reshaping the squad—Trent Alexander-Arnold's move to Real Madrid, along with the departures of Thiago Alcantara, Joel Matip, and others in previous windows—Liverpool's attacking structure relies more heavily on current personnel. Salah's sustained excellence therefore becomes even more critical.

Virgil van Dijk's defensive imperatives and Alisson Becker's presence between the posts provide the defensive platform upon which Salah's attacking brilliance is built. This holistic statistical picture demonstrates why Liverpool remains competitive under Slot's management.

## Conclusion

The statistical evidence unequivocally establishes Mohamed Salah as the Premier League's most complete attacking threat in the 2025-26 season. His 33 goal contributions, combined with his defensive work rate and creative progression, represent elite-level performance. As Liverpool's campaign develops, Salah's continued output will likely prove decisive in determining the club's silverware prospects.

For those questioning Salah's impact at Anfield, the data provides definitive answers: he remains irreplaceable.
