# Dominik Szoboszlai's Creative Mastery: A Statistical Deep Dive into Liverpool's Midfield Engine

As Liverpool FC continues to navigate the 2025-26 campaign under Arne Slot's stewardship, few players have proven as instrumental to the team's attacking philosophy as Dominik Szoboszlai. The Hungarian international has evolved into the creative heartbeat of Slot's midfield, and the statistical evidence underscores just how vital his contributions have become to Liverpool's offensive output.

## The Shot-Creating Actions Revolution

Shot-creating actions—defined as the two offensive actions directly leading to a shot—have become a crucial metric for evaluating attacking midfielders and creative full-backs. Szoboszlai's proficiency in this area has been nothing short of remarkable.

| Metric | Szoboszlai (2025-26) | Liverpool Midfield Avg | Premier League Avg (Mid) |
|--------|----------------------|------------------------|-------------------------|
| Shot-Creating Actions per 90 | 4.8 | 3.2 | 2.9 |
| Shot-Creating Actions (Total) | 118 | — | — |
| Conversion Rate (SCA to Shot) | 34% | 28% | 26% |
| Accurate Pass Rate | 91% | 86% | 84% |

These figures demonstrate Szoboszlai's elite standing within Liverpool's structure. His 4.8 shot-creating actions per 90 minutes places him comfortably above both his teammates and the Premier League average for midfielders. More impressively, when Szoboszlai initiates a sequence that leads to a shot, the conversion rate stands at 34%—a testament to the quality of his positioning and decision-making alongside the finishing prowess of Mohamed Salah, Darwin Nunez, and Luis Diaz.

## Positional Heat Map and Creative Zones

Szoboszlai's effectiveness stems partly from his positioning. Operating primarily from the left-centre and centre-right areas of midfield, he covers approximately 11.8 kilometres per match—among the highest in the squad alongside Ryan Gravenberch.

His creative output is concentrated in three zones:

- **Central Corridor (40%)**: Direct play through the middle for Nunez and Alexis Mac Allister's runs
- **Right Flank (35%)**: Delivering to Salah in wide and inverted positions
- **Left Channel (25%)**: Switching play to Diaz and supporting attacking left-back rotations
