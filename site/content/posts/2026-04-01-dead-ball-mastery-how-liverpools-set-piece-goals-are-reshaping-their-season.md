# Dead-Ball Mastery: How Liverpool's Set-Piece Goals Are Reshaping Their Season

**Wednesday, 1 April 2026**

Under Arne Slot's management, Liverpool FC has undergone a tactical transformation that extends far beyond open play. The 2025-26 campaign has witnessed a remarkable shift in how the Reds manufacture goals from set pieces—a statistical trend that warrants deeper examination as the season reaches its climax at Anfield and beyond.

## The Set-Piece Revolution

Historically, Liverpool has been perceived as a team that thrives in transition and open-play scenarios. However, this season's data reveals a calculated, systematic approach to dead-ball situations that rivals some of Europe's most organised defences-turned-attackers. The metrics suggest that Slot's coaching staff has invested considerable time in both offensive and defensive set-play routines.

Through 34 matches in the 2025-26 season, Liverpool has registered **18 goals directly from set pieces**—corners, free-kicks, and throw-ins combined. This represents approximately 28% of the club's total goal output, a significant proportion that demonstrates the tactical emphasis placed on these situations.

## The Defensive Colossus: Virgil van Dijk

No player exemplifies Liverpool's set-piece prowess more than **Virgil van Dijk**. The Dutch centre-back has scored **6 goals from set pieces this season**—all from corners—a remarkable tally for a defender. Van Dijk's aerial dominance in the box has become a weapon that opposition managers must account for in their planning. His 94% success rate in aerial duels during set-play situations underscores his value in these scenarios.

Alongside van Dijk, **Ibrahima Konate** has contributed 3 set-piece goals, whilst **Joe Gomez** has added 1 from defensive positions. The partnership between van Dijk and Konate represents a significant strategic asset, with defenders accounting for **10 of Liverpool's 18 set-piece goals**.

## Attacking Contributions

Whilst defensive contributions dominate the set-piece goalscoring ledger, the attacking contingent has also benefited. **Mohamed Salah** has scored 3 goals from set pieces—primarily from free-kick situations where his technical quality provides an advantage. **Darwin Nunez** has netted 2 set-piece goals, both headers from corners, whilst **Cody Gakpo** and **Luis Diaz** have each registered 1.

## Defensive Solidity from Set Pieces

Equally impressive is Liverpool's defensive record from dead-ball situations. Conceding only 4 set-piece goals in 34 matches represents elite-level defending. **Alisson Becker** has been instrumental, with his commanding presence and distribution reducing opposition opportunities. The goalkeeper's ability to claim high balls under pressure has prevented numerous dangerous situations from developing into clear-cut chances.

The partnership between Alisson and the defensive line—featuring van Dijk, Konate, Gomez, and **Jarell Quansah**—has created a cohesive unit that understands positioning and zoning principles critical to set-play defence.

## Tactical Implementation Under Slot

Aerne Slot's emphasis on set-play structure is evident in Liverpool's training ground work. The recruitment of players with aerial prowess, combined with the coaching staff's detailed analysis of opposition routines, has created a multifaceted approach. Corner delivery has improved markedly, with **Ryan Gravenberch** and **Harvey Elliott** providing consistent, weighted service from wide areas.

## Conclusion

As Liverpool navigates the remainder of the 2025-26 campaign, the set-piece statistics paint a picture of a team that has evolved tactically under Slot's management. With 18 goals from dead balls and a near-impenetrable defensive record in these situations, the Reds have discovered an additional dimension to their attacking armoury. Whether this trend sustains through crucial fixture congestion will significantly impact their ability to compete for silverware at Anfield and beyond.
