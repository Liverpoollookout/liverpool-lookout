# On This Day: The Mohamed Salah Signing That Changed Liverpool FC Forever

**22 March 2026** – Today, we look back not to this exact date, but to the summer of 2017, when Liverpool FC made a decision that would fundamentally reshape the club's trajectory for nearly a decade. The arrival of Mohamed Salah wasn't just a transfer; it was the catalyst for a renaissance that would define an entire era.

## A Club in Transition

When Jürgen Klopp took over at Anfield in October 2015, Liverpool FC was a giant stirring from a prolonged slumber. The club had tasted Premier League titles – the last in 1990 – and European glory, but the intervening years had been characterised by inconsistency and, at times, genuine mediocrity. By the summer of 2017, Klopp had laid foundations, but the squad needed a statement signing. Someone who could ignite the imagination of the fanbase and demonstrate the manager's ambition.

Enter Mohamed Salah, then 25 years old, returning to the Premier League after spells at Chelsea and Roma. Few could have predicted just how profound his impact would become.

## The Arrival

The fee – reported to be in the region of £36 million – seemed substantial at the time, though hardly record-breaking. Salah had shown glimpses of his quality in Serie A, but the Premier League had previously proved challenging during his time at Stamford Bridge. There were whispers of doubt. Some questioned whether Klopp had overpaid for a player who'd struggled in England before.

Those doubts evaporated almost immediately.

## Revolutionising the Attack

What Salah brought to Anfield transcended mere goalscoring statistics, though those would prove extraordinary. He arrived with a specific intensity and intelligence that complemented Klopp's high-pressing philosophy perfectly. The Egyptian international was technically sublime – his left foot a weapon of devastating precision – but more importantly, he possessed an insatiable hunger that aligned precisely with the manager's demands.

In that 2017-18 season, Salah scored 32 Premier League goals, an astonishing tally that announced his arrival as one of Europe's elite forwards. But the real magic lay in how he transformed Liverpool's attacking identity. Alongside Roberto Firmino and Sadio Mané, Salah completed a trinity that would terrorise defences across Europe for years to come.

## Building Towards Glory

The Salah signing wasn't made in isolation. It was part of a carefully constructed plan. Klopp and sporting director Michael Edwards had identified the gaps in the squad and moved to address them systematically. Yet Salah's arrival seemed to unlock something within the team. The confidence was palpable. Here was a player of genuine world-class calibre choosing to join Liverpool's project, validating Klopp's vision and raising expectations throughout the club.

Over the following seasons, that investment would yield extraordinary returns. The 2018-19 Champions League victory – that unforgettable night in Madrid when Salah scored against Tottenham Hotspur in the final – represented the pinnacle of what Klopp's team could achieve. Yet Salah's contributions extended far beyond individual moments of brilliance. He was the constant thread running through Liverpool's most successful period in the modern era.

## A Legacy Extending Beyond Goals

Now, nearly nine years later, with Arne Slot at the helm and the club embarking on a new chapter, Salah remains a central figure at Anfield. His continued presence speaks volumes about his adaptability and professionalism. Here was a player who could have commanded moves to the world's biggest clubs on multiple occasions, yet remained committed to Liverpool FC.

The Egyptian has become far more than a footballer at Liverpool. He represents a specific moment when the club dared to be ambitious again, when Klopp's gamble on collective unity and relentless pressing could attract the world's finest talent. Salah's signing sent a message: Liverpool was back.

## The Ripple Effect

What's remarkable about the Salah transfer is how it altered Liverpool's entire approach to recruitment. Success breeds confidence, and the confidence generated by Salah's impact allowed the club to make further bold moves. Virgil van Dijk's arrival in January 2018 transformed the defence. Alisson Becker followed. The midfield was revolutionised with signings that prioritised energy and technical quality.

None of this would have unfolded with quite the same momentum without Salah's immediate success. He was the breakthrough moment – the sign that Liverpool could attract and retain world-class talent in a way that had seemed impossible during the post-Ferguson period when Manchester rivals dominated.

## Reflection and Continuity

As Liverpool navigates the Slot era, the club carries forward the principles established during that remarkable period. Current squad members like Virgil van Dijk, Alisson Becker, and the emerging talents around them stand on the shoulders of giants. Salah's legacy isn't simply inscribed in goal tallies – though those remain staggering – but in a fundamental shift in how Liverpool conducts itself as a club.

The signing of Mohamed Salah in summer 2017 was the moment Liverpool FC reminded the world of its true potential. And that, quite simply, changed everything.
