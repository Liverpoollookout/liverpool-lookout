# Liverpool Face Major Summer Upheaval: Salah Departure Signals Potential Reds Rebuild

**Sunday, 29 March 2026** — Liverpool FC braces for significant squad changes this summer, with Mohamed Salah's anticipated departure from Anfield marking the most seismic shift in Arne Slot's early tenure at the club.

According to recent reports, Salah will leave Liverpool at the end of the 2025-26 season, bringing an end to one of the most prolific eras in modern Reds history. The Egyptian winger's impending exit has forced Slot and the Anfield hierarchy into contingency planning mode, with multiple positions requiring attention ahead of next season.

## Salah's Exit Forces Strategic Rethink

The loss of Salah represents far more than the departure of a single player. His goal-scoring prowess from the right flank has been fundamental to Liverpool's attacking identity for nearly a decade. With the 33-year-old set to leave, Slot must now identify creative alternatives to maintain the Reds' attacking threat.

Cody Gakpo, currently occupying a wide role in Liverpool's attack, is likely to feature prominently in Slot's revised plans. However, sources suggest the manager may seek additional reinforcement in wide areas, particularly if speculation regarding other departees proves accurate.

## Contract Situations Under Scrutiny

Beyond Salah's confirmed departure, several other contract situations demand immediate attention. Virgil van Dijk and Alisson Becker, both cornerstone figures in Liverpool's defence and goalkeeping ranks respectively, will enter the final year of their current agreements this summer. The club must resolve their long-term futures to avoid potential summer departures.

Dominik Szoboszlai, arguably the most important midfielder in Slot's setup, has also become a subject of transfer speculation according to reports. However, Liverpool sources indicate the Hungarian midfielder remains central to the manager's vision, suggesting contract discussions are progressing positively.

## Defensive Reinforcement Required

With Joel Matip and Trent Alexander-Arnold having departed since the 2023-24 campaign, Liverpool's defensive ranks require careful management. Ibrahima Konate and Joe Gomez have stepped up admirably, whilst Jarell Quansah continues his development. Nevertheless, Slot is reportedly exploring options to strengthen the backline, particularly given the advancing age of Van Dijk.

Konstantinos Tsimikas and Conor Bradley provide left-back depth, though their respective futures remain uncertain. Reports suggest Liverpool may target experienced full-back reinforcements during the summer window to bolster this critical area.

## Midfield Depth Concerns

Ryan Gravenberch and Alexis Mac Allister have performed admirably in midfield this season, yet the departure of senior figures including Thiago Alcantara, Fabinho, and Jordan Henderson in recent years has depleted squad experience. Wataru Endo offers reliability, whilst Curtis Jones represents emerging talent, but Slot may seek additional midfield support.

Harvey Elliott's development trajectory will also influence midfield strategy. The young midfielder continues establishing himself at Anfield, with potential for significant growth under Slot's tutelage.

## Attacking Options Beyond Salah

Darwin Nunez, Luis Diaz, Federico Chiesa, Diogo Jota, and Cody Gakpo form a formidable attacking contingent. Slot must orchestrate these talents effectively following Salah's departure, potentially reconfiguring tactical approaches to maximise their collective potential.

Chiesa's arrival represented ambitious recruitment, and his integration alongside the aforementioned attackers suggests Slot possesses creative flexibility. However, sources suggest the manager may require additional attacking depth, particularly if outgoings extend beyond Salah.

## Summer Window Outlook

Liverpool's 2026 summer transfer window promises considerable activity. Beyond managing Salah's departure and resolving contractual matters, Arne Slot faces the challenge of maintaining competitive standards whilst potentially overseeing the most significant rebuild since the managerial transition that brought Jürgen Klopp to Anfield.

Fans and analysts await confirmation regarding Van Dijk and Alisson's new deals, which will prove crucial in determining the scope and ambition of Liverpool's summer recruitment strategy. The coming weeks promise intrigue at Anfield.
