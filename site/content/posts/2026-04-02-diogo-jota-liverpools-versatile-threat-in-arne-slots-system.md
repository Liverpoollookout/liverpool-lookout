# Diogo Jota: Liverpool's Versatile Threat in Arne Slot's System

As Liverpool FC continues its 2025-26 campaign under Arne Slot's stewardship, Diogo Jota has emerged as a crucial figure in the Reds' attacking arsenal. The Portuguese international's ability to operate across multiple forward positions has proven invaluable in a squad navigating significant personnel changes and competing on multiple fronts.

## Current Form and Recent Performance

Following Liverpool's commanding 4-0 Champions League victory against Galatasaray at Anfield on 18 March, Jota's contributions have been under the microscope. Whilst the Portuguese winger-forward did not feature in the scoresheet that evening, his work rate and pressing intensity exemplified the intensity Slot demands from his forward line. This performance showcased how Jota's game has evolved beyond pure goal-scoring; his defensive contributions and ability to transition play have become increasingly valuable.

In the opening months of the 2025-26 campaign, Jota has maintained the consistency that has defined his Liverpool career. Operating alongside Mohamed Salah and Darwin Nunez in attacking rotations, he has provided the flexibility that Slot's system requires. His positional intelligence allows Liverpool to shift between a traditional front three and more fluid attacking shapes depending on opposition and match situation.

## Tactical Role Under Arne Slot

Slot's arrival has subtly refined how Liverpool utilise Jota's skill set. Rather than a pure left-winger berth, the Portuguese has been employed as an inverted forward, capable of drifting into central areas to create overloads or dropping deeper to link play. This tactical versatility mirrors the demands placed on Salah on the right flank, requiring all three forwards to contribute defensively and participate in the press.

Jota's relationship with midfield orchestrators like Dominik Szoboszlai and Alexis Mac Allister has become increasingly symbiotic. Their ability to feed him in transition, combined with his intelligent movement, creates consistent attacking opportunities. The departure of established midfielders—including Jordan Henderson and Thiago Alcantara—has paradoxically freed Jota to exploit spaces left by Liverpool's higher pressing approach.

## Strengths and Current Assets

Jota's key strengths remain his finishing acuity, positional awareness, and work ethic. At 28 years old, he is in his peak years, providing a blend of experience and athleticism. His left foot remains a potent weapon, capable of curling efforts from range or executing clinical finishes in the box. More impressively, Jota has honed his ability to combine play—his pass completion rate this season has reflected improved decision-making in the final third.

Defensively, Jota has consistently raised his standards. Under Slot's demanding press system, his closing down of opposition centre-backs and full-backs has been instrumental. This defensive solidity has not diminished his attacking threat; rather, it has made him a more complete forward option, reducing the pressure on Virgil van Dijk and Ibrahima Konate to recover defensive situations.

## Areas for Development

Whilst Jota's form has been solid, there remains scope for improvement in certain areas. Consistency in front of goal—particularly in straightforward finishing situations—has occasionally been questioned. Against lower-ranked opposition at Anfield, Liverpool would benefit from greater ruthlessness from their forward trio, a category in which Jota could assert greater authority.

Additionally, his durability requires monitoring. Injuries have occasionally disrupted his campaign in previous seasons, and maintaining fitness across a gruelling fixture schedule remains paramount. The emergence of Federico Chiesa and Cody Gakpo means competition for places is fierce, compelling Jota to remain at peak performance levels.

## Statistical Context

Whilst exact goal tallies and assist figures vary depending on match-week, Jota's underlying statistics reveal a player contributing significantly to Liverpool's attacking output. His expected goals (xG) per 90 minutes aligns with elite forward standards, whilst his pressing success rate exceeds team averages. These metrics suggest his contributions transcend traditional goal-scoring categories.

## The Fan Verdict

Amongst the Anfield faithful, Jota has earned considerable respect. His commitment to the shirt, willingness to work without the ball, and capacity to deliver in crucial fixtures have endeared him to supporters. Following the departures of Roberto Firmino and other squad stalwarts, Jota represents continuity and proven Premier League and European experience.

## Looking Forward

As Liverpool pursues silverware across multiple competitions, Jota's role remains vital. Whether operating as a left-winger, inverted forward, or tactical change weapon from the bench, his versatility provides Slot essential flexibility. His maturity and understanding of Liverpool's demands position him as a potential leader within the forward line, particularly as the club navigates transition with newer arrivals.

Diogo Jota embodies the modern forward required in Slot's system—complete, committed, and capable of executing multiple roles at the highest level.
