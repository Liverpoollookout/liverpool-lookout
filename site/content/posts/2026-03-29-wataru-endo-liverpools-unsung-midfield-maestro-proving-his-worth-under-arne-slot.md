# Wataru Endo: Liverpool's Unsung Midfield Maestro Proving His Worth Under Arne Slot

Whilst Mohamed Salah continues to dominate headlines with speculation surrounding his future, and Darwin Nunez captivates supporters with his explosive attacking prowess, one Liverpool FC player has been quietly conducting affairs in the engine room with exemplary professionalism: Wataru Endo.

The Japanese midfielder has become an increasingly vital cog in Arne Slot's Liverpool machine during the 2025-26 campaign, establishing himself as the steadying presence Liverpool require in midfield following the departures of seasoned veterans including Jordan Henderson and Fabinho in preceding seasons.

## The Evolution of Endo's Liverpool Career

Since his arrival at Anfield, Endo has endured a transition period that many overlooked. However, under Slot's stewardship, the midfielder has flourished, demonstrating the kind of positional intelligence and defensive acuity that underpinned Liverpool's most successful periods.

The Champions League victory against Galatasaray on 18 March—a commanding 4-0 triumph at Anfield—showcased precisely why Endo has become indispensable. Operating alongside Ryan Gravenberch and Alexis Mac Allister in midfield, Endo provided the defensive shield that allowed Liverpool's attacking talents to express themselves freely. His positioning prevented Galatasaray from establishing any meaningful rhythm, breaking up play before it threatened Alisson Becker's goal.

## Tactical Mastery and Role Definition

Under Slot's tactical framework, Endo has evolved into a modern defensive midfielder—far removed from the stereotypical destroyer. His role demands constant circulation of possession, intelligent pressing triggers, and an understanding of when to shield the backline featuring Virgil van Dijk and Ibrahima Konate.

What distinguishes Endo's contribution is his reading of the game. Whilst others might react to Liverpool's opponents, Endo anticipates their movements. He intercepts passes that hadn't reached their intended destination, breaks up attacks in their infancy, and transitions possession with remarkable efficiency for a midfielder operating with primarily defensive responsibilities.

Against technical sides—precisely the opposition Liverpool face in European competition—Endo's ability to win the ball cleanly and distribute it to creative talents like Dominik Szoboszlai and Harvey Elliott has proven invaluable. He provides the platform upon which Liverpool's attacking philosophy thrives.

## Statistical Contribution and Form

During the 2025-26 campaign, Endo's statistics reveal a player operating at peak efficiency. His tackle success rate remains among Liverpool's highest, his pass completion percentage hovers comfortably above 85%, and crucially, his positioning has reduced the defensive burden placed upon Van Dijk and Konate.

The midfielder also demonstrates impressive ball recovery statistics, frequently winning possession in advanced areas that spark dangerous Liverpool counterattacks. Curtis Jones and other attacking midfielders benefit considerably from Endo's metronomic presence, allowing them to venture forward with defensive security.

His contribution extends beyond statistics. Endo provides emotional leadership—a quality often undervalued in modern football analysis. He organises those around him, communicates relentlessly, and represents the professional standards Slot has installed throughout the squad.

## Strengths and Development Areas

Endo's greatest strength remains his positional discipline and reading of play. He rarely lunges into challenges, instead positioning himself to intercept or block passing lanes. This intelligence prevents Liverpool from conceding the chaotic, poorly-structured goals that plague less organised sides.

His passing range has also improved noticeably. Rather than simply recycling possession backwards, Endo increasingly threads passes into advanced areas, supporting Liverpool's transition game and maintaining momentum.

If areas for improvement exist, they centre on occasionally heightened aggression in winning aerial duels—a dimension where taller midfielders occasionally capitalise. Additionally, his long-range shooting remains an underutilised weapon, though such offensive contributions remain secondary to his primary responsibilities.

## The Unsung Hero Narrative

In a Liverpool squad containing multiple world-class attacking talents, Endo rarely receives the plaudits his performances merit. Fans celebrate Salah's brilliance, Nunez's intensity, and Luis Diaz's directness—justifiably so. However, without Endo's meticulous midfield control, these attacking jewels cannot flourish.

Acknowledging Endo's value requires appreciating football beyond headline statistics and aesthetic appeal. His contribution operates in the unglamorous realm of possession reclamation, dangerous space elimination, and tempo management. These elements don't generate viral social media moments, yet they fundamentally determine whether Liverpool wins trophies.

## Fan Verdict and Future Prospects

Amongst Liverpool's support base, Endo has cultivated considerable respect. Supporters recognise his consistency, professionalism, and indispensability to Slot's project. During challenging matches, his calm presence provides reassurance.

Looking forward, Endo remains central to Liverpool's Champions League aspirations and domestic title pursuit. Provided he maintains his current performance trajectory and remains available for selection, the midfielder will continue proving that unglamorous excellence constitutes the foundation upon which silverware is secured.

As speculation swirls around Salah's future and summer transfer windows loom, Wataru Endo represents something valuable at Liverpool: a player content to excel in his defined role, asking nothing more than the opportunity to contribute meaningfully to the club's success. In Arne Slot's Liverpool, that dedication deserves considerably greater recognition.
